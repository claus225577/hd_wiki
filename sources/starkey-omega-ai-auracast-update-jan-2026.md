# Starkey Omega AI Auracast and Feature Updates

**Rollout:** January 2026 (widely rolled out)

## Key Features

- **Auracast Assistant:** Starkey's implementation of Bluetooth LE Audio Auracast broadcast reception — enables Omega AI hearing aids to receive Auracast broadcasts in venues
- **Google Fast Pair:** Seamless Android pairing, matching Oticon's Verit and Play SI integration
- **Push to Talk:** Dedicated communication mode; likely enables quick voice capture for assistant/communication features
- **Respiratory rate tracking:** Adds another health biometric to Starkey's health monitoring suite alongside:
  - Heart rate monitoring
  - Fall detection (existing)
  - Activity tracking (existing)

## Significance for Hearing AI

- **Auracast adoption broadening:** Starkey joining Oticon and ReSound with Auracast support means 3 of 6 major manufacturers now ship Auracast-capable devices — approaching critical mass
- **Health monitoring expansion:** Respiratory rate is a clinically meaningful vital sign:
  - Abnormal respiratory rate is an early indicator of respiratory distress, heart failure, and infection
  - Combined with heart rate and activity data, enables more comprehensive health profiling from the ear
  - Strengthens Starkey's "health tech company" positioning
- **Google Fast Pair standardization:** Both Starkey and Oticon now offering Google Fast Pair indicates Android ecosystem integration becoming table stakes
- **Push to Talk:** Suggests Starkey is moving toward voice-first interaction paradigms for hearing aids
