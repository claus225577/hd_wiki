# Sterkens Joins Center for Hearing Access

## Bibliographic
- **Person:** Dr. Juliëtte Sterkens, AuD
- **Organization:** Center for Hearing Access (CHA), founded 2024
- **Role:** Hearing Access Advocate
- **Effective:** 1 Jun 2026
- **URL:** https://hearingreview.com/inside-hearing/people/juliette-sterkens-joins-center-for-hearing-access

## What
Sterkens — a Dutch-born audiologist with 45+ years of clinical and advocacy experience — joins the Center for Hearing Access (CHA) full-time as Hearing Access Advocate. Track record:
- Drove the Wisconsin hearing-loop rollout (1,000+ venues looped).
- Recipient of the American Academy of Audiology Presidential Award.
- 1.3 M+ views on her TEDx talk "What You Don't Know About Hearing Aids."

CHA is a national non-profit that educates consumers, audiologists, hearing-instrument specialists, and facility staff on ADA-compliant assistive listening systems (loops, FM, IR, and now Bluetooth LE Audio / Auracast).

## Why It Matters
This appointment is structural, not personnel. Three readings:
1. **CHA is consolidating as the canonical US ALS advocacy hub** right as Auracast public-venue deployment hits scale. A single recognizable advocate gives venue operators and policymakers one named entity to consult.
2. **Loop-era → Auracast-era transition is being staffed.** Sterkens built her career on hearing loops. Her pivot to a Bluetooth-LE-Audio-era advocacy role signals that the loop community is folding into the next generation of ALS rather than fighting it.
3. **Consumer-side AI hearing-aid messaging is going to come through this channel.** A 1.3 M-view TEDx benchmark is the kind of reach OEMs do not have access to.

## Context
- HLAA 2026 Convention this week (10-12 Jun) is the natural amplification surface for CHA's positioning.
- Lands alongside the Auracast venue trials in London (National Theatre Dorfman, Bridge Theatre) and Android 16 native Auracast support.
