# AirPods Pro 3: Better Today Than at Launch

## Key Extractions
- AirPods Pro 3 now has **FDA-approved hearing aid feature** for mild-to-moderate hearing loss
- Price: **$200** (lower than AirPods Pro 2's $249 launch)
- **Heart rate monitoring** via LEDs pulsing **256 times per second** through in-ear optical sensors
- **Clinical-grade hearing test** built into iPhone — no audiologist visit required
- **Active hearing protection** — real-time monitoring of sound exposure levels
- **Conversation Boost** — directional amplification for face-to-face conversations
- **67% more battery life in Transparency mode** compared to previous generation
- **Continuous software updates** — Apple regularly adding features post-launch, improving device over time
- Apple converging consumer earbuds with medical hearing devices and health monitoring in a single $200 product

## Relevance to Hearing + AI
- **Price disruption deepens:** AirPods Pro 3 at $200 is now cheaper than even ultra-affordable OTC hearing aids like Ceretone (£130/$165). A clinical-grade hearing aid for $200 from the world's most valuable company fundamentally disrupts pricing
- **Health platform convergence:** Heart rate monitoring + hearing aid + hearing test + hearing protection in one device mirrors Starkey's health-in-hearing-aids strategy but at consumer electronics scale (hundreds of millions of units vs. millions)
- **Software-defined hearing aid:** Continuous post-launch improvements via software updates contrast with traditional hearing aids that ship with fixed firmware
- **Battery improvements** (67% more in Transparency mode) address one of the key limitations vs. dedicated hearing aids
- **PPG heart rate at 256 Hz** — higher sampling rate than many dedicated health wearables, suggesting Apple is serious about clinical-grade health monitoring from the ear canal
