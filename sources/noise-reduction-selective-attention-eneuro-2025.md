# Noise Reduction Improves Neural Tracking of Selective Attention in HA Users

## Summary

Investigates how hearing aid noise reduction and nonlinearity compensation affect EEG-based tracking of selective auditory attention. Published in eNeuro, February 2025.

## Key Contributions

- Shows that hearing aid noise reduction improves neural tracking of attended speech
- Nonlinearity compensation also contributes to better attention tracking
- Bridges hearing aid signal processing and neurosteering research
- Practical implications: better HA processing → better AAD performance → better neurosteering

## Significance

Connects two previously separate research streams: hearing aid signal processing and auditory attention decoding. Finding that HA noise reduction improves AAD performance means that as hearing aids get better DNN-based noise reduction, neurosteering systems will also work better — a virtuous cycle.
