# AirPods Pro 2 Hearing Aid Feature vs. Prescribed Targets — Clinical Audit

## Citation
PubMed-indexed within the last 24 hours (full bibliographic details to be filled in once the abstract is retrievable). Surfaced via The Hearing Wire's PubMed feed on 27-28 Jun 2026.

## Key Idea
Independent verification study of how closely Apple's AirPods Pro 2 OTC Hearing Aid mode matches **standard prescriptive targets** (NAL-NL2, DSL v5) across a panel of standardised audiogram configurations (e.g., flat, sloping, reverse-slope, ski-slope HF losses).

## Why It Matters for Hearing AI
- **First independent clinical-grade audit of the AirPods feature** since Apple's December 2025 launch. Until now, target-match claims have been largely vendor-sourced (Apple Hearing Study readouts) or anecdotal.
- **Bridges the regulatory framing.** FDA's OTC category requires self-fitting effectiveness; matching prescriptive targets is the closest objective proxy for "effectiveness" the field has.
- **Sets a benchmark** other OTC entrants (Jabra Enhance, Sony C20, Cearvol Wave, etc.) will be measured against. Expect a wave of similar audits in the next 6-12 months.
- **Defines the gap** between consumer-tech entrants and prescription devices in a quantifiable way — the missing piece in the BIHIMA-style "channel-shift" surveys.

## Open Questions / Things to Watch
- Which audiogram shapes does the AirPods fit best / worst? (Reverse-slope and steep HF losses are typically where consumer fits fall apart.)
- How is the fit derived — pure-tone in-app audiogram vs. clinician-uploaded audiogram — and does derivation method change the match quality?
- Does iOS 26 firmware 8A356 (Conversation Boost) change steady-state fit, or only environment-driven adjustments?
