# HLAA 2026 Convention — Louisville

## Bibliographic
- **Organization:** Hearing Loss Association of America (HLAA)
- **Event:** 41st Annual HLAA Convention
- **Dates:** 10–12 June 2026
- **Venue:** Galt House Hotel, Louisville, Kentucky
- **Primary URL:** https://www.hearingloss.org/get-involved/hlaa-convention/
- **Secondary URL:** https://hearingreview.com/inside-hearing/events/hlaa-2026-convention-to-convene-in-louisville

## Accessibility Substrate

- **Real-time captioning** provided for every plenary session and every workshop.
- **Hearing loops** installed in every meeting room.
- Both are operated and consumed at scale by the actual end-user community — making the convention one of the largest live stress tests of consumer accessibility tech each year.

## Friday Research Symposium — "The AI Revolution in Hearing Health: Smarter Tech, Greater Access"

- **When:** Friday June 12, 10am CT
- **Support:** NIDCD-supported
- **Frame:** the first time an HLAA Research Symposium has billed AI as the headline topic.

The symposium is the centerpiece signal that AI in hearing health has crossed from engineering/clinical conferences into the patient-side discourse — the audience here is end-users and advocates, not chip engineers.

## Why This Matters

### Patient-voice convention complements the engineering/clinical thread
- **HEAL 2026 Padua (4-6 June 2026)** — clinical/scientific
- **arXiv-week (early June 2026)** — engineering
- **HLAA 2026 Louisville (10-12 June 2026)** — patient/advocacy

For the first time in 2026 all three rungs of the discourse ladder are talking about AI in hearing health in the same fortnight. HLAA is the patient-side counterpart to the European HEAL thread and the arXiv flow covered in early June.

### First major US patient-side convention with AI as a first-class topic
Previous HLAA conventions have treated AI as a technology vendor topic in the expo hall. The 2026 Friday Symposium puts AI in the research-plenary slot — the slot reserved for what the community considers worth weighing as policy and personal-choice information.

### Living test of the accessibility stack
Every meeting room hearing loop + every session captioned = the largest annual real-world deployment test of:
- Auracast / T-coil interoperability at consumer scale
- Real-time captioning latency and accuracy at conference scale
- Mixed wearer/non-wearer accessibility expectations in 2026

### Policy adjacency
- Coincides with the Veterans Hearing Aid Improvement Act push (HLAA is endorsing on both House and Senate versions).
- HLAA's membership calls Congress. Whatever the Friday Symposium frames as "delivered AI value" vs "AI hype" gets translated into constituent positions within weeks.
