# Real-time Control of a Hearing Instrument with EEG-based Attention Decoding

## Summary

Landmark paper demonstrating real-time EEG-based neurosteering of a hearing instrument. The system decodes which speaker a hearing-impaired listener is attending to and selectively enhances that sound source. Published February 2025 in Journal of Neural Engineering.

## Key Findings

- Demonstrated real-time closed-loop system: EEG signals → attention decoder → hearing aid beamforming adjustment
- System works with hearing-impaired listeners, not just normal-hearing subjects
- Advances neurosteering from lab concept toward practical hearing aid feature
- Researchers from Technical University of Denmark and international collaborators

## Significance

This is one of the first demonstrations of a complete closed-loop neurosteered hearing device. Previous work largely operated offline or with normal-hearing subjects. The transition to real-time with hearing-impaired users is a critical milestone for the neurosteering hearing aid concept.

## Related Research

- TSF-AADNet (2025): Novel neural framework for short-window auditory attention decoding using temporal-spatial and frequency-spatial dual-branch architectures
- Wireless EEG sensor network (2026): Two-node, four-channel setup for practical miniaturized neurosteered hearing devices (bioRxiv)
- In-ear EEG comparison (2025, Nature Scientific Reports): Direct comparison of scalp, around-ear, and in-ear EEG for neural selective auditory attention decoding
