# FDA Approves Otarmeni (lunsotogene parvec-cwha) — First Gene Therapy for Genetic Hearing Loss

## Key Facts
- **Drug:** Otarmeni (lunsotogene parvec-cwha), manufactured by Regeneron Pharmaceuticals
- **Indication:** Severe-to-profound sensorineural hearing loss caused by biallelic OTOF gene mutations
- **Mechanism:** Dual AAV (adeno-associated virus) vector-based gene therapy injected into inner ear fluid (perilymph); delivers functional copies of the otoferlin gene to restore inner hair cell synaptic transmission
- **Approval date:** April 23, 2026
- **Approval pathway:** National Priority Voucher program — approved in just 61 days (vs. typical 10-12 months)
- **Efficacy:** 80% of patients experienced hearing improvement, defined as ≤70 dB HL at 24 weeks post-treatment
- **Pricing:** Regeneron providing Otarmeni free of charge in the United States
- **Historical significance:** First-ever FDA-approved gene therapy for any form of hearing loss

## Technical Details
- OTOF gene encodes otoferlin, a protein critical for synaptic vesicle fusion at inner hair cell ribbon synapses
- Biallelic OTOF mutations cause DFNB9 — accounts for ~2-8% of prelingual severe-to-profound sensorineural hearing loss
- Dual AAV vector approach required because the OTOF coding sequence (~6 kb) exceeds single AAV packaging capacity (~4.7 kb)
- Two AAV vectors carry split halves of the otoferlin gene; recombine in target cells via homologous recombination
- Single injection into perilymphatic space during surgical procedure

## Significance
- Validates gene therapy as a viable treatment modality for genetic hearing loss
- OTOF is one of ~200 genes known to cause congenital deafness — potential pipeline for additional gene therapies
- The 61-day approval via National Priority Voucher demonstrates regulatory willingness to fast-track hearing therapeutics
- Free US pricing removes economic barrier and sets precedent for gene therapy accessibility
- Shifts hearing loss treatment paradigm from management (hearing aids, CIs) to potential cure for genetic forms
