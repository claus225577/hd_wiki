# VCCA 2026 Pre-Conference Workshops

Three workshops ran in the lead-up to VCCA 2026 (main conference June 25–26). Workshops 2 and 3 were on Friday, June 19, 2026 and constitute the first time the audiology community's flagship virtual conference has run hands-on machine-learning and computational-modelling labs as foundational pre-conference fare.

## Workshop 1 — Research & Scientific Communication in Audiology
- **Date:** Thursday, June 18, 2026 @ 18:00 Madrid / 12:00 Miami
- **Presenters:** Dr. Natàlia Gorina-Careta (Universitat de Vic — UCC, Spain); Dr. Helia Relaño-Iborra (Eriksholm Research Centre, Denmark; University of Rochester)
- **Title:** "From Question to Evidence: Building Your Research and Scientific Communication in Audiology"

## Workshop 2 — Machine Learning for Hearing Science
- **Date:** Friday, June 19, 2026 @ 16:00 Madrid / 10:00 Miami
- **Presenter:** Dr. Mark Saddler (DTU Hearing Systems, Technical University of Denmark; ex-McDermott lab MIT)
- **Title:** "A Practical Introduction to Machine Learning for Hearing Science"
- **Format:** Hands-on Jupyter notebooks; materials on GitHub. Python/MATLAB familiarity helpful but not required.

## Workshop 3 — Computational Models of the Auditory Periphery
- **Date:** Friday, June 19, 2026 @ 18:00 Madrid / 12:00 Miami
- **Presenter:** Annesya Banerjee (Harvard University; MIT)
- **Title:** "Computational Models of the Auditory Periphery: From Normal Hearing to Impairment"
- **Format:** Interactive simulations; no coding experience required. Materials on GitHub.

## Key Extraction

VCCA 2026's pre-conference offering is the first time a hands-on ML lab + a computational-auditory-periphery lab appear together in the foundational tutorial slot of the audiology community's annual virtual conference. The discipline-formation signal: where 5 years ago an audiologist who wanted ML went to INTERSPEECH or NeurIPS as a guest, in 2026 the audiology community itself runs the foundational lab — taught by audiology-systems researchers, not borrowed from a CS department. Combined with main-conference Session 4A (ML & AI for Hearing Science, chaired by Prof. Dr. Tobias Goehring) and Prof. Enrique A. Lopez-Poveda's keynote ("Computational Auditory Models in the Service of Improving Audiology"), the conference treats computational models as substrate, not sidecar.

Saddler's workshop also signals the public face of his research program (see [[../wiki/concepts/task-optimized-dnn-as-auditory-model]]): training task-optimized DNNs on biophysically faithful cochlear simulations such that the trained model becomes a candidate hypothesis about how human hearing works.
