# VCCA2024 — 5th Virtual Conference on Computational Audiology

**Dates:** June 20–21, 2024
**Format:** Fully virtual, free
**Abstracts:** 89 submitted abstracts
**Chair:** Simone Graetzer (University of Salford)
**Co-chairs:** Trevor Cox (Salford), Jon Barker (Sheffield), Jan-Willem Wasmann (Radboud)
**Scientific Committee Chair:** Volker Hohmann (University of Oldenburg)

## Keynote Speakers

1. **Deniz Başkent** (University of Groningen) — "New directions for diagnostic and rehabilitation tools"
2. **Manohar Bance** (University of Cambridge) — "Could we make cochlear implants work better?"
3. **Melanie Ferguson** (Curtin University) — "From nice-to-have to must-have: the essential role of co-design and participatory approaches in digital hearing healthcare"
4. **Lars Bramsløw** (Eriksholm Research Centre) — "State of the art and future developments in hearing aid technology"

## Featured Talks

- **Ariane Laplante-Lévesque** — Improving access to hearing assistive technology
- **Anthea Bott** — Optimizing manufacturing of custom hearing aids
- **Harvey Dillon** — Causes of deficits in sentence understanding in noise
- **Mareike Buhl & Charlotte Vercammen** — Big data and data standards in audiology
- **George Tilston** — Curating routinely collected hearing-health data using OMOP framework
- **Siobhan Brennan** — The Audiology Learning Disabilities and Autism Project
- **Emma Holmes** — Directing selective attention in noisy environments
- **Andrew Hugill** — Aural Diversity introduction
- **Malcolm Slaney** — Bayesian Models of Speech Understanding

## Session Topics

- Modelling hearing and hearing loss
- Novel research related to AI and hearing
- Field-related measurement and validation
- Speech enhancement for hearing device wearers
- Cochlear, brainstem and vestibular implants
- Cognitive and neuro-audiology
- Translating academic research into clinical practice
- ISCA accessibility and inclusive design session

## Key Extraction

VCCA2024 marked significant growth with 89 abstracts — the largest VCCA to date. Lars Bramsløw's keynote on HA technology future directions and Manohar Bance's talk on improving CIs were standout industry-relevant talks. The OMOP framework talk (Tilston) signals movement toward standardized clinical data in audiology. Harvey Dillon's work on sentence-in-noise deficits bridges computational modelling and clinical reality. All pitch videos available on YouTube.
