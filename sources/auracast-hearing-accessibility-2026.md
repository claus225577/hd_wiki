# Auracast — The Hearing Accessibility Infrastructure Gap

Analysis of Bluetooth LE Audio's Auracast broadcast feature and its adoption status in the hearing aid industry.

## What is Auracast
- Bluetooth LE Audio broadcast feature allowing one audio source to broadcast to unlimited hearing aids simultaneously
- No pairing required, no neck loops, no special equipment at venues
- Enables direct audio streaming from PA systems, airport gates, lecture halls to hearing aids

## Adoption Status (as of April 2026)
- **Hearing aids ready:** ReSound Vivia and Nexia already support Auracast
- **Venues lagging:** Very few public venues have installed Auracast transmitters
- **ABI Research forecast:** Bluetooth SIG projects meaningful venue adoption by 2029

## The Gap
- Technology is ready on the device side but infrastructure lags ~3 years behind
- This is a data/incentive problem, not a technology problem
- Venues lack clear ROI metrics for accessibility investments
- No standardized way to measure Auracast usage or impact

## Implications
- Opportunity for hearing aid companies to drive venue adoption through partnerships
- Data on usage patterns could help build the business case for venues
- Potential for hearing aid companies to provide turnkey Auracast installation kits
