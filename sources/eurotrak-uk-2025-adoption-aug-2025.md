## Key Facts

- UK hearing aid adoption rate fell to 50.5% in 2025, down from 52.8% in 2022 — first reported dip in years.
- UK still ranks third globally among 10 EuroTrak countries.
- Despite the adoption dip, satisfaction and usage metrics rose:
  - Overall satisfaction: 82% (up from 75% in 2022)
  - App adoption among wearers: 31% (up from 22%)
  - App satisfaction: 76% (up from 65%)
  - Daily wear time: 9 hours (up from 8.1 hours)
  - Quality-of-life improvement reported: 96%
  - Stigma reduction (never feel mocked): 70% (up from 64%)
- Top non-adopter reasons (persistent over time):
  - Denial of hearing loss
  - Perceived discomfort
  - Perceived lack of severity ("hear well enough")
- Report frames hearing instruments as "intelligent health devices" featuring "AI, real-time sound processing, and app-based controls" — yet adoption still drops.
- Critical gap acknowledged: "misperceptions and lack of information continue to hold people back."

## Why It Matters

EuroTrak UK 2025 is the cleanest *consumer-survey* data point in 2025/2026 showing the decoupling between satisfaction (which AI features can move) and adoption (which they apparently cannot). The 2.3-percentage-point drop in adoption while satisfaction climbed 7 points isolates the bottleneck to top-of-funnel factors: awareness, denial, perceived severity, friction at the door.

Pair with the EHIMA 2025 global sales report (+2.1% unit growth, decelerated from +7.7% in 2023) for a coherent decoupling story at both the unit-shipment and survey levels.

For data scientists in hearing care: the unbuilt models are at the funnel, not at the chip. Marketing analytics, propensity scoring, channel attribution, and friction-mapping are the underbuilt layer.
