# Audiologist Workforce Shortage

## Key Extractions

- **Geographic coverage gap:** **75% of US counties** are audiologist shortage areas
- **Travel burden disparity:** Rural patients travel **68 minutes** on average vs. **32 minutes** for urban patients to reach an audiologist
- **Profession growth:** 9% growth projected over 2024-2034 decade (~1,500 new jobs total)
- **Structural mismatch:** Growth rate not keeping pace with aging population — the 65+ cohort is growing ~3% annually while the audiologist workforce grows <1% per year
- **Source data:** ASHA (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association) supply-demand workforce data combined with ScienceDirect spatial analysis of audiologist distribution

## Significance for Hearing + AI

- The workforce shortage is a primary structural driver for AI-assisted fitting, teleaudiology, and OTC self-fitting solutions
- 75% of counties being shortage areas means technology solutions are not optional — they're required for adequate coverage
- The 68 min vs. 32 min travel gap creates a concrete equity dimension: rural and underserved communities bear disproportionate access burden
- AI-powered automated audiometry, remote fitting, and self-fitting algorithms directly address this bottleneck
- OTC hearing aids partially bypass the audiologist bottleneck but create new risks (under-treatment, mis-fitting) without professional oversight
- Teleaudiology can extend one audiologist's reach across many shortage counties, but requires broadband + digital literacy
