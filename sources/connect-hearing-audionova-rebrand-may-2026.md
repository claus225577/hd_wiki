# Connect Hearing → AudioNova (US Rebrand, May 2026)

## Summary
Sonova announced that its US retail chain **Connect Hearing** (160+ centres) is being folded into the global **AudioNova** brand. The transition is positioned as a 2-year process designed to consolidate Sonova's global retail footprint under a single banner.

## Key Facts
- Announced 1 May 2026 (HearingTracker coverage).
- US locations affected: ~160+ Connect Hearing centres.
- AudioNova has been Sonova's European retail brand since the 2016 acquisition; this brings the US business onto the same brand.
- 2-year transition period (full unification target ~2028).

## Strategic Read
- Sits alongside the announced **Sennheiser consumer-hearing divestiture** — both are part of Sonova's push to consolidate around its core hearing-care lanes (Phonak/Unitron + Advanced Bionics) and shed non-core consumer audio.
- Reinforces the **vertical integration** trend in the industry: manufacturers increasingly own the retail funnel (Demant ↔ HearingLife, WSA ↔ HearUSA, Amplifon-as-distributor, Sonova ↔ AudioNova).
- Potential signal that Sonova is preparing for tighter integration between device telemetry (Phonak myPhonak, DEEPSONIC data) and retail-side fitting/care workflows under one brand.

## Caveats
- "Process designed to help consolidate" implies the rebrand may extend over multiple quarters; not all 160+ stores flip immediately.
- US clinic-level reaction (audiologist retention, local brand equity loss) is not yet reported.

## Relevance to Hearing + AI
- Retail consolidation matters for AI/data strategy: a unified brand allows centralized fitting-data pipelines, A/B program rollouts, and cleaner consent workflows for ML training data.
- Combined with **AAA 2026 panel** signals about AI integration, this looks like infrastructure-prep for AI-driven retail audiology.
