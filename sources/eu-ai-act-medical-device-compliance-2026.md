# EU AI Act Medical Device Compliance Timeline

## Key Facts
- EU AI Act Class IIa medical device provisions entering compliance period in 2026
- AI-powered hearing aids classified as medical devices fall under Class IIa
- Manufacturers must comply with transparency, risk management, and documentation requirements for AI/ML components
- Affects all hearing aid manufacturers selling AI-powered devices in the EU market

## Significance for Hearing AI
- **First binding AI regulation** affecting hearing aid DNN systems — transforms AI from a marketing feature to a regulated component
- **Class IIa classification** means hearing aids with AI are considered "medium risk" medical devices under EU AI Act
- Compliance requirements likely include:
  - Documentation of training data, model architecture, and validation procedures
  - Risk assessment for AI-driven clinical decisions (e.g., automatic scene classification affecting amplification)
  - Transparency requirements — users may need to be informed when AI is making processing decisions
  - Post-market monitoring of AI performance
- **Impact on OTA model updates:** Firmware updates that change DNN behavior may require re-certification or conformity assessment
- Could slow the pace of DNN model iteration for EU-marketed devices vs. unregulated markets
- Manufacturers with stronger documentation practices (Sonova clinical trials, Demant research publications) may have an advantage
- OTC hearing aids with AI features also affected if marketed as medical devices in the EU

## Strategic Implications
- May accelerate the separation between "AI hearing aids" (regulated, Class IIa) and "AI earbuds" (consumer electronics, less regulated)
- Could drive consolidation: smaller manufacturers may lack regulatory resources to comply
- Documentation requirements align with the clinical evidence trend (Phonak NCT07526428 trial)
