# AONDevices × P-Logic — Microwatt Edge AI for Hearables (CES 2026)

## Summary

Joint CES 2026 demonstration of always-on edge-AI silicon engineered for the microwatt-class power budgets required by hearables, safety tags, and continuous-sense wearables. Targets multi-modal inference (voice + ambient sound classification + multi-sensor fusion) without cloud round-trips.

## Why It Matters for Hearing Aids

- **Removes the "AI hearing aid" rhetorical gap.** Most current consumer-marketed "AI hearing aid" features still rely on phone-side compute or coarse on-DSP heuristics. Microwatt-class always-on inference on the device itself is the actual technical bar that distinguishes marketing copy from architecture.
- **Multi-sensor fusion for context.** Hearing-aid signal processing benefits sharply from non-acoustic context: motion (user walking? in vehicle?), orientation, biometric state. AONDevices framing makes that fusion the chip's first-class operating mode.
- **Pairs with the dedicated-AI-chip thesis.** Co-designed with the Phonak Sphere Infinio (2024) and Fortell Spatial AI (2025–2026) lineage — the silicon-first design strategy now has multiple commercial-stage entrants.

## Technical Profile

- Always-on inference at microwatt-range power consumption — compatible with hearing-aid battery envelopes.
- Multi-modal: voice, sound, motion, sensor fusion.
- Targets "always-on safety tags and wearables" as primary market — hearables an explicit secondary application.
- P-Logic complement adds packaging / system-integration partnership.

## Open Questions

- Whether the AONDevices stack is targeting hearing-aid OEM design wins or own-brand hearable plays.
- Pricing / availability for OEM evaluation kits not publicly stated.
- Latency budget for real-time speech enhancement at this power point not disclosed in CES materials.
