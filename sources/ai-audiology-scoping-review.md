# Scoping Review of AI in Audiology 2020-2024

## Key Extractions
- Review covers 91 studies from 2020-2024, representing 87.5% of all AI-in-audiology publications to date
- Deep learning and cochlear implants identified as the two motor (dominant, established) themes
- Emerging themes identified: high-frequency audiometry, tinnitus, hearing aid fitting, and hearing screening tools
- LLMs being applied to automatic diagnostic report generation in audiology clinical workflows
- Key challenges identified: data privacy constraints, small dataset sizes, algorithmic bias, lack of clinical validation pathways
- Rapid acceleration of publication rate — the field nearly doubled its total output in 4 years
- Cochlear implant programming and outcome prediction are among the highest-activity sub-areas
- Tinnitus and high-frequency audiometry emerging as next growth areas

## Relevance
Provides the field map for where AI is active in audiology. The 87.5% publication concentration in 2020-2024 confirms this is a fast-moving, recently ignited field. Identifying tinnitus and HF audiometry as emerging themes is useful for anticipating the next research wave.
