# Rethinking the Audiologic Evaluation — FHH 2026 Panel

## Bibliographic
- **Event:** Future of Hearing Healthcare (FHH) 2026 — panel session
- **Date:** 8 Jun 2026 (recorded / reported)
- **Coverage URL:** https://hearinghealthmatters.org/thisweek/2026/rethinking-audiology-testing-modern-practice/

## What
A panel of audiology leaders publicly argued that the traditional pure-tone-audiometry (PTA)-anchored test battery is no longer sufficient for contemporary patient populations. Topics framed as the next standard of care:
- Speech-in-noise testing (e.g., QuickSIN, AzBio, DIN tests).
- Otoacoustic emissions (OAEs) as routine pre-screen.
- Central auditory screening (suprathreshold and cognitive overlay).
- Teleaudiology as a standard delivery mode, not an exception.
- AI-driven diagnostic workflows (automated interpretation, suspect-flagging, longitudinal phenotyping).

## Why It Matters
This is the consensus signal AI-first diagnostic platforms have been waiting for. The reimbursement gate on AI-driven audiology workflows is conditional on the field publicly agreeing that the existing test battery is incomplete. A leadership-panel framing at FHH 2026 is the kind of citable artifact CPT-code-revision and AAA position-statement processes need.

Implications:
- Companies like Neurotone AI, VCCA, and the broader computational-audiology stack get a credible "the field is moving" reference.
- OEM diagnostic platforms (Demant's Audiometer Affinity, etc.) get pressure to integrate central/SIN/OAE modules natively.
- Teleaudiology and longitudinal phenotyping platforms get a tailwind.

## Context
- Lands the same week as HLAA 2026's Friday Research Symposium on AI in hearing health — both events are pushing the same "AI-driven workflows" frame from different sides (clinical vs. consumer).
- Aligns with the Auditdata June 2026 survey on the audiologist-time crisis: more diagnostic depth is only viable if AI absorbs the interpretation overhead.
