# Scalp vs. Around-Ear vs. In-Ear EEG for Auditory Attention Decoding

## Summary

First direct, simultaneous comparison of three EEG recording modalities — scalp, around-ear, and in-ear — for neural selective auditory attention decoding to speech. Published in Nature Scientific Reports.

## Key Findings

- Compared all three modalities recorded simultaneously from the same listeners
- Evaluated decoding performance across modalities for fair comparison
- In-ear EEG is most practical for hearing aid integration but has fewer channels and lower SNR
- Around-ear EEG offers a middle ground between practicality and signal quality

## Significance

Critical benchmarking study for the neurosteering hearing aid field. If in-ear EEG can achieve sufficient AAD accuracy, it could be integrated directly into earbuds or hearing aids without any visible external electrodes — a key requirement for consumer adoption.
