# Signia Active Mini IX (May 12, 2026)

## Summary
On May 12, 2026 Signia (WS Audiology) launched the **Active Mini IX**, billed as the world's smallest **ready-to-wear / instant-fit in-the-ear (ITE) hearing aid** delivering the full audiological performance of the Signia **Integrated Xperience (IX)** platform.

## Key Specs
- **Form factor:** ready-to-wear (instant-fit) ITE — no custom mold, no manufacturing wait
- **Platform:** Signia Integrated Xperience (IX) — full performance
- **Signal processing:** RealTime Conversation Enhancement (RTCE), two-microphone directional system
- **Battery:** rechargeable, up to **21 hours per charge**, fast charging
- **Connectivity:** iPhone + Android streaming, Signia Assistant in-app
- **Colors:** Garnet Black, Rose Gold, Pearl White
- **Internal research:** 97% comfort satisfaction

## Strategic Significance
- **Closes the impression-to-fit gap.** Custom ITEs take 1–2 weeks between ear-impression and patient pickup. Active Mini IX collapses that to same-day fitting.
- **Data-pipeline implication:** First acoustic / motion / wear-time / app-interaction telemetry begins on fit-day rather than weeks later, when patient motivation has decayed. Signia Assistant — the AI app feature that takes real-world adjustment requests — gets its first user signal from hour one.
- **Demographic expansion:** Reaches cosmetic-sensitive first-time users who would otherwise drop out of the funnel while waiting for a custom shell.
- **Training-data distribution:** ITE deep-canal acoustic phenotype differs from BTE/RIC; BTE-derived data has dominated training datasets for two decades. Faster ITE proliferation broadens the acoustic distribution feeding personalization models.
- **Form-factor follow-on to the Sound Preference Program (Apr 2026)** and **Lexie B3 (May 2026)** — WSA is layering on top-of-funnel awareness, mid-priced OTC, and now instant-fit premium ITE within ~6 weeks.

## Executive Quote
> "Discreet, ready-to-wear hearing aids have traditionally required trade-offs."
> — Jose Alves, head of Signia

## Limits / Open Questions
- True effect on **time-to-first-telemetry** is unmeasured externally; WSA has not (yet) published telemetry-onset distributions vs. custom-ITE controls.
- Comfort claim (97%) is internal research; independent validation pending.
- Acoustic performance vs. custom-molded ITE under deep-fit conditions is not characterized in launch materials.
