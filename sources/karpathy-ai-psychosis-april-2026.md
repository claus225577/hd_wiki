# Karpathy "AI Psychosis" Concept

## Key Extraction

- **Author/Speaker:** Andrej Karpathy (former Tesla AI lead, OpenAI co-founder)
- **Concept:** "AI Psychosis" — the growing gap in AI understanding between tech workers and the general public
- **Date:** April 9-11, 2026 (various coverage dates)
- **Core argument:**
  - Developers who work with AI daily have developed a mental model of AI capabilities and limitations that is dramatically different from public perception
  - The public either dramatically overestimates AI (fear of AGI/superintelligence) or dramatically underestimates it (dismisses as a chat toy)
  - This perception gap creates problems for product adoption, regulation, and trust
  - "Psychosis" metaphor: the two groups are experiencing fundamentally different realities regarding the same technology
- **Significance for Hearing AI:**
  - Hearing aid industry faces this exact gap: manufacturers market "AI hearing aids" to consumers who may not understand what the AI actually does (DNN noise reduction ≠ conversational AI)
  - Audiologists (the middle layer) must translate AI marketing into patient-understandable benefits
  - The "Auracast Ready vs Enabled" confusion is a micro-example of this broader understanding gap
  - Risk: AI marketing in hearing aids could trigger backlash if patients expect conversational AI but get (excellent) noise reduction
  - Opportunity: companies that bridge the understanding gap with clear, honest communication about what their AI does will build more trust
  - Connects to Karpathy's earlier "idea files" paradigm for AI agent development (previously ingested)
