# Wasano et al. — Tinnitus CBT App RCT (JAMA Otolaryngology, May 2026)

## Summary

Double-blind, sham-controlled randomized clinical trial of a prototype digital therapeutic mobile app delivering educational counseling and cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) for chronic tinnitus. Conducted at Tokai University School of Medicine (Japan). Published in *JAMA Otolaryngology–Head & Neck Surgery*, May 2026.

## Design

- **Type:** Double-blind, sham-controlled RCT
- **N:** 60 patients with mild-to-severe chronic tinnitus (30 therapeutic arm, 30 sham)
- **Intervention:** 16 weeks of active app use; therapeutic functions blocked for 8-week follow-up (weeks 16–24)
- **Sham comparator:** identical-looking app with therapeutic content disabled
- **Primary outcome:** Tinnitus Handicap Inventory (THI) score change

## Headline Results

- **Between-group THI improvement (therapeutic vs sham):**
  - **−20.4 points at week 16**
  - **−18.3 points at week 24** (i.e., effect persisted 8 weeks after therapeutic functions were blocked)
- **Adverse events:** none serious reported
- **Direction of effect:** consistent with prior iCBT-for-tinnitus literature; effect size larger than is typical for app-based trials in this space

## Why It Matters

- **Methodological rigor:** double-blind + sham-controlled is the highest bar in digital therapeutics trials. The sham comparator addresses the long-standing "is this just engagement / attention effect?" critique.
- **Durability signal:** ~9% loss of effect across the 8-week blocked-functions follow-up suggests skills generalize, not just usage-coupled symptom suppression.
- **Reimbursement implication:** the kind of evidence package required to push CBT-tinnitus apps from "wellness" labeling toward reimbursable SaMD (FDA, MHLW, EU MDR).
- **Effect-size context:** **-20.4 THI points** is ~3× the canonical 7-point THI minimum clinically important difference (MCID). Even allowing for sample-size inflation, the signal is large.

## Contrast With SilentCloud RWD (Schmitt & Stropahl 2026)

| Dimension | Wasano RCT (this paper) | SilentCloud RWD |
|---|---|---|
| Design | Double-blind RCT, sham control | Retrospective observational |
| N | 60 (controlled exposure) | 17,271 registered → 63 completers |
| Primary effect (THI) | −20.4 between-group | −14.7 within completers |
| Completion / adherence | Controlled trial cohort | ~0.4% completion |
| Evidence layer | Efficacy | Real-world effectiveness + adherence |

The two papers are complementary: Wasano establishes that the protocol works under controlled conditions; SilentCloud quantifies the funnel-collapse problem at scale. Adherence is the bridge between them.

## Limitations

- N=60 is small; effect estimate likely overstated relative to a Phase 3-scale replication
- Single-center, single-country (Japan) — generalizability open
- App name not disclosed in early press coverage; appears to be a research prototype, not a commercial product
- Sham comparator quality is decisive in interpreting effect size; methodology details warrant a close read in the full paper
