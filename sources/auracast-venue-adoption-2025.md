## Key Facts

- Early venue adopters of Auracast broadcast audio include Sydney Opera House, CCI London, Oslo Central Theater, St Paul's Cathedral, and CES 2025 exhibitors.
- Android ecosystem support expanding: Samsung One UI 7 devices and Pixel 9 with Android 16 include native Auracast receiver support.
- ABI Research projects 30M+ Bluetooth hearing aids shipping annually by 2029, creating a large installed base for Auracast-compatible devices.
- Auracast enables hearing aid wearers to receive venue audio streams directly in their devices without assistive listening loops or FM systems.
- ReSound Vivia (2025) among first hearing aids with native Bluetooth LE Audio + Auracast support.
- Eliminates historic reliance on aging hearing loop (telecoil) infrastructure — venues can broadcast to any compatible receiver regardless of brand.
- Latency performance of BLE Audio suited to live entertainment use cases; multi-stream audio allows different language tracks in the same space.
