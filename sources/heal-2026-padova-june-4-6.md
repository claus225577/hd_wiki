# HEAL 2026 — Hearing Across the Lifespan

## Key Facts
- **Dates:** 4–6 June 2026 (in progress at time of ingest)
- **Venue:** Padova Conference Centre, Padova (Padua), Italy
- **Scale:** ~230 oral presentations, ~100 posters, 5 parallel rooms, 40+ countries
- **Format:** Special Sessions, Panel Discussions, Keynote Lectures, regular oral + poster tracks
- **Series:** HEAL has run biennially for over 20 years; the previous edition (2024) was in Cernobbio, Italy

## Significance for AI / Data Science

### Track-Structure Shift
For the first time in the HEAL series, **two AI/computational tracks sit in the regular main program**, alongside the canonical clinical tracks (Speech in noise testing, Listening effort, Cognitive decline, etc.), rather than being relegated to a single special session.

The implication: HEAL's organising committee — which represents the clinical/audiological mainstream — has decided that AI and computational methods are no longer adjacent to the field, but inside it.

### Co-occurrence With June 2026 arXiv Wave
HEAL 2026 runs in the same week as four arXiv drops that share a structural move — pulling the perceptual / clinical model into the optimisation loop:
- **arXiv:2606.04103** — Ballesta Rosen et al., *The Differentiable Auditory Loop (DAL)* — Google Research Australia + Macquarie, 4 Jun 2026. CARFAC cochlear model in JAX inside the training loop.
- **arXiv:2606.04221** — Olalere et al., *Feasibility of Time-Domain DNN-Based Speech Enhancement on Embedded FPGA for Hearing Aid* — Radboud Donders + Columbia Zuckerman, 4 Jun 2026. First peer-reviewable FPGA latency benchmark.
- **arXiv:2606.03832** — Voit & Doclo, *In-the-Loop Training of Deep Feedback Cancellation for Hearing Aids* — Oldenburg, 3 Jun 2026. Closed-loop training of DFC.
- **arXiv:2606.02305** — Ciferri et al., *Mapping Whisper Representations to Human ECoG Responses* — ICLR 2026 Workshop on Representational Alignment, 1 Jun 2026. Intermediate Whisper layers map to auditory-cortex ECoG.

### Cross-Disciplinary Vocabulary
The two-decade translational gap between "clinical hearing outcome" and "ML loss function" has historically meant the two communities published in different journals and met at different conferences. HEAL 2026's track structure and the simultaneous arXiv wave together suggest the gap has finally narrowed enough that the rooms can hear each other.
