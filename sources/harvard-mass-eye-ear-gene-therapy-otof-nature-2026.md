# Harvard/Mass Eye and Ear OTOF Gene Therapy Study — Nature, April 2026

## Key Facts
- **Published in:** Nature (April 22, 2026)
- **Institutions:** Mass Eye and Ear, Harvard Medical School, Fudan University
- **Target:** OTOF gene mutations causing congenital deafness
- **Results:** 90% of recipients saw hearing improve; half reached normal hearing levels at 2.5-year follow-up
- **Age effect:** Patients 18 and under saw strongest gains
- **Follow-up:** 2.5 years — longest published follow-up for hearing gene therapy

## Significance
- Demonstrates durability of hearing gene therapy results over multi-year timeframe
- 50% achieving normal hearing levels is a remarkable outcome for severe-to-profound genetic hearing loss
- Age correlation (younger = better outcomes) suggests earlier intervention may be optimal
- Published same week as FDA Otarmeni approval — represents convergence of clinical evidence and regulatory action
- OTOF is one of ~200 genes known to cause congenital deafness — pipeline potential enormous
- Validates international collaboration model (Harvard + Fudan) for hearing gene therapy research

## Connection to Otarmeni
- Both target OTOF gene mutations
- This study provides longer-term follow-up data (2.5 years vs. 24-week FDA endpoint)
- Suggests Otarmeni outcomes may continue to improve beyond the 24-week FDA assessment window
