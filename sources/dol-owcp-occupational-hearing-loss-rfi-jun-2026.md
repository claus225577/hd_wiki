# DOL OWCP — Public-Input Window on Occupational Hearing-Loss Evaluation

## Event
The US Department of Labor's Office of Workers' Compensation Programs (OWCP) opened a public-input window seeking comment on whether current methods for evaluating occupational hearing loss remain the most reliable and appropriate. The framing explicitly opens the door to "objective hearing tests alongside traditional audiograms" — i.e., OAE, ABR, and speech-in-noise measures alongside (or as alternatives to) pure-tone audiometry.

## Why It Matters for Hearing AI
- **First serious federal signal** that the workers-compensation framework — historically anchored on pure-tone audiograms via AMA Guides — may evolve toward objective and supra-threshold measures.
- **Hidden hearing loss** (cochlear synaptopathy) is invisible on a standard audiogram but produces functional speech-in-noise deficits. Any move toward speech-in-noise as a compensable measure would dramatically expand the population recognised as having work-attributable hearing damage. Connects directly to `wiki/concepts/hidden-hearing-loss.md` and `wiki/syntheses/hidden-hearing-loss-fullstack-june-2026.md`.
- **Reimbursement implications for AI hearing aids.** A broader definition of compensable loss → larger reimbursable population → larger addressable market for the AI-enabled hearing-aid stack.
- **Pulls AI-based clinical-decision-support into scope.** ML-based audiogram analysis, automated OAE interpretation, and AI scoring of speech-in-noise tests all become more strategically interesting if OWCP guidelines start to formalise multi-modal assessment.
- **Sets up a federal-vs-state divergence.** Most states have already started experimenting with speech-in-noise carve-outs; OWCP guidance would harmonise.

## Open Questions / Things to Watch
- Window length and comment-period deadline.
- Whether the RFI scopes only the OWCP federal-worker programs or signals a Labor-wide rethink (which would push into AMA Guides revision).
- Stakeholder coalitions — HIA / ASHA / AAA positions vs. industry-side OTC entrants.
- Knock-on effects for the Veterans Hearing Aid Improvement Act pipeline (`veterans-hearing-aid-improvement-act-house-jun-2026.md`) — VA and OWCP frameworks are adjacent.
