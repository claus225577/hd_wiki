# AI Predicts Spoken Language Outcomes in Deaf Children Post-CI

## Key Extractions
- AI model predicts spoken language development in deaf children following cochlear implantation with 92% accuracy
- Prediction uses pre-implant brain MRI data — neuroimaging before surgery informs outcome forecasting
- Deep transfer learning methodology applied to MRI analysis
- Dataset: 278 children across 3 languages — Cantonese (Hong Kong), English (Australia), Spanish (US)
- Multilingual and multi-site design strengthens generalizability of findings
- Model outperforms traditional clinical predictors (demographics, audiological history)
- Early prediction enables more targeted rehabilitation planning and parental counseling
- Suggests neuroplasticity markers visible in MRI are predictive of language learning success post-implant

## Relevance
92% accuracy from pre-implant MRI is a clinically actionable finding. Enables risk stratification and personalized rehab planning before implantation. The multilingual, multi-site scope makes this one of the most generalizable CI outcome prediction studies to date.
