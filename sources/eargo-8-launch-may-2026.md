# Eargo 8 — May 2026 OTC Launch

## Summary

Eargo launched the Eargo 8 in mid-May 2026 — its first product release since merging with LXE Hearing. CIC (completely-in-canal) form factor, FDA-cleared OTC, MSRP $2,699. Two headline features distinguish it from prior Eargo generations and competing OTC offerings:

1. **Smart Sound Adjust** — environmental classification system that analyzes ambient noise in real time and automatically engages fast-acting noise reduction without user input (enters "noisy restaurant" mode automatically). Functionally a scene classifier + auto-program-switch, ported into an OTC price point.
2. **Device Scan** — a patented acoustic-signature self-check that uses the in-canal speaker/microphone path to detect wax or other physical blockages and alerts the user. First widely-deployed in-ear earwax/occlusion detector marketed as a wellness feature rather than a fitting workflow step.

## Key Specs

- **Form factor:** Completely-in-canal, discreet
- **Battery:** 16 h per charge; case provides ~14 recharges (≈2 weeks total runtime)
- **Self-fitting:** Improved self-fitting flow (details not yet third-party verified)
- **Price:** $2,699 MSRP (premium-tier OTC)
- **Distribution:** Direct-to-consumer, FDA OTC
- **Lineage:** Successor to Eargo 7; first launch under post-LXE merged entity

## Significance

### OTC Premium Tier Holding Its Ground
Pricing at $2,699 keeps Eargo in the premium OTC segment alongside Apple's HAF-on-AirPods, Lexie B3 ($999), and the JLab/Audien mass-retail tier. Eargo is betting CIC discreteness + automation features justify the price floor that consumer-electronics OTC entrants (Sony, Bose-via-Lexie) have been compressing.

### Scene Classification Moves Down-Market
Smart Sound Adjust ports a feature that prescription HAs have used for two decades (Phonak AutoSense, Oticon MoreSound Intelligence, Signia AI-Soundscape) into a self-fitting OTC product. The technical content is unlikely to match DEEPSONIC / Polaris R-class chips, but the *user-facing claim* is now equivalent — "the device adapts to your environment automatically" — which collapses one of the prescription tier's marketing differentiators.

### Device Scan = Telemetry Through the Speaker
The earwax-detection feature uses the speaker+microphone pair as a sensor — sending a known acoustic probe and analyzing the reflected signature. This is the same architectural pattern as in-ear PPG / heart-rate sensors but applied to canal occlusion. Worth watching whether this generalizes into broader in-canal sensing (canal volume changes, eardrum reflectance, otitis screening) — Eargo claims a patent on the approach.

### LXE Merger Output
First product since the Eargo + LXE Hearing merger. Validates the merged entity is shipping rather than restructuring; positions Eargo for a 2026 H2 push.
