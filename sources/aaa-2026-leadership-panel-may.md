# AAA 2026 Industry Leaders Panel

## Summary
At the AAA 2026 convention, executives from **Sonova, Demant North America, GN/ReSound, Starkey, and Cochlear America** convened on a panel forecasting the future of hearing care. Two themes dominated: **AI integration as table-stakes** and **the audiology workforce shortage**.

## Key Points
- **AI is no longer differentiating, it's expected.** Panelists treated AI integration as a baseline requirement for any 2026+ hearing-aid product, not a premium feature.
- **Workforce shortage is structural.** Not enough new audiologists are graduating to replace retirees; panel framed this as the industry's most pressing operational risk over a 5-year horizon.
- **Reframe hearing loss as a core health issue.** Consensus push to position hearing loss alongside cognition, falls, and isolation — not as a quality-of-life or lifestyle concern.
- **Career-path advocacy.** Panel agreed the industry must do more to surface diverse and rewarding career paths available with an audiology degree (clinical, research, industry, regulatory).

## Strategic Read
- The panel's unified framing of "AI is now baseline" suggests the next round of differentiation will be in **clinical evidence, fitting workflows, and post-fitting care** — not in raw model capability.
- Workforce shortage + AI integration is a logical pairing: AI tools (auto-fitting, remote monitoring, decision support) become a partial response to fewer audiologists per patient.
- The "core health issue" framing aligns with the IHS Better Hearing Month proactive-care push and the recent ACHIEVE-style cognitive-decline evidence.

## Caveats
- Panels are PR-adjacent; concrete commitments (e.g., funding for audiology programs) were not detailed in the coverage.
- "AI is baseline" claim conflicts somewhat with the AI-understanding-gap synthesis already in the wiki — distinction worth tracking is product-level AI vs. organization-level AI literacy.
