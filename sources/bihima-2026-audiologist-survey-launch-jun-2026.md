# BIHIMA Launches 2026 UK & Ireland Audiologist Survey

## What

The **British Irish Hearing Instrument Manufacturers Association (BIHIMA)** has opened its **2026 annual audiologist survey** for UK and Ireland hearing-care professionals across all practice settings (NHS, independent, multiple/chain, manufacturer-owned). Builds on the 2024/2025 edition, which highlighted growing patient demand, workforce pressure, and the need for system-wide reform.

## Survey Focus

This year's instrument explicitly pulls on:

- **Workforce-shortage signals** — staffing pressure, time-loss, and capacity.
- **AI tooling in clinical workflow** — how (or whether) AI is being used in fitting, triage, documentation.
- **Patient demand shifts** — including OTC / consumer-electronics encroachment (AirPods Hearing Aid feature, Auracast venues).
- **System-reform priorities** — what audiologists most want changed.

## Why It Matters

- **First UK/Ireland counterpart to the US Auditdata time-crisis instrument** ([[../sources/auditdata-audiologist-time-survey-jun-2026.md]]) — together they form a transatlantic measurement pair for the workforce-shortage and back-office-AI theses being tracked under [[../wiki/concepts/audiologist-workforce-shortage.md]].
- **BIHIMA reports annually**, so the 2026 edition will be directly comparable to 2024/2025 baselines — adoption-rate tracking of AI tooling in UK clinics becomes a citable longitudinal signal.
- **NHS-vs-independent split** is uniquely visible in the BIHIMA instrument (US has no direct analogue). Whether NHS audiologists report systematically slower AI-tool adoption than independents would be a meaningful structural finding.
- **Pairs naturally with the BIHIMA Q1 2026 sales report** (+12.5% QoQ, [[../sources/bihima-q1-2026-uk-sales-jun-2026.md]]) — demand-side and workforce-side instruments from the same trade body, same reporting cycle.

## Carry-Forward Flags

- Aggregated 2026 results publication date — becomes a citable benchmark for the workforce page.
- Whether 2026 introduces new questions on Auracast-readiness in clinics (could be the first quantitative read on operator-side LE Audio deployment).
- AI-tooling-adoption delta vs 2024/2025 — will indicate whether the AAA-2026 back-office-AI thesis is materialising in field-clinic workflow.
