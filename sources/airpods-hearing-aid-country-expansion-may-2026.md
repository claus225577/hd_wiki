# AirPods Hearing Aid Feature Expanded to Italy, Romania, Czechia — May 2026

## Key Extractions

- **Date:** 12 May 2026 (announced by Apple)
- **What expanded:** AirPods Pro 2 and AirPods Pro 3 Hearing Aid feature is now available in **Italy, Romania, and Czechia**.
- **Bundled in same release:** Apple Watch hypertension alerts also extended to these markets.
- **Stack requirements:** AirPods Pro 2 or Pro 3 paired with a device running iOS 26 / iPadOS 26 or later.
- **Global footprint:** Hearing Test and Hearing Aid features are now available in **more than 100 countries and regions**, making AirPods one of the most widely distributed hearing-aid form factors in the world by sheer geographic reach.

## Geographic Rollout Context

- The feature first launched in late 2024 in the U.S. following FDA clearance and has been gradually expanded as regional regulatory approvals come through.
- A December 2025 release added Cyprus, Czechia, France, Italy, Luxembourg, Romania, Spain, UAE, and the UK (Cyprus, Czechia, France, Italy, Luxembourg, Romania, Spain, UAE per iOS 18.2 wave was the original spec; the May 12 update finalizes Italy, Romania, and Czechia on the iOS 26 channel).
- Each expansion requires local medical-device regulatory clearance — the cadence reflects EU MDR and national-competent-authority processes more than Apple engineering work.

## Why This Matters

1. **Distribution leverage:** Geographic expansion of an FDA-cleared OTC hearing aid feature without any clinician in the loop is structurally novel — no incumbent has distribution remotely comparable.
2. **EU acceleration:** Adding Italy, Romania, and Czechia keeps Apple's EU footprint expanding into markets that have historically had low hearing-aid penetration (Romania in particular has limited prescription HA infrastructure).
3. **Bundled health story:** Co-releasing with Apple Watch hypertension alerts reinforces Apple's hearing-as-healthspan strategy — see [[../concepts/longitudinal-hearing-phenotyping]] and [[../concepts/hearing-loss-dementia-link]].
4. **Competitive pressure:** Each new country adds millions of consumers who can purchase a clinical-grade hearing aid for $200 without a clinic visit. Local prescription incumbents (e.g., Amplifon Italy) face accelerated OTC competition.
