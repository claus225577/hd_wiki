# VCCA2025 — 6th Virtual Conference on Computational Audiology

**Dates:** June 26–27, 2025
**Format:** Fully virtual, free, no registration fees
**Organization:** Computational Audiology community

## Keynote Speakers

1. **Dr. Giovanni Di Liberto** — "Investigating auditory cognition with natural speech and music"
2. **Dr. Louise Hickson** — "Exploring the social connections of adults with acquired hearing loss"
3. **Dr. Dorothea Kolossa** — "Audio-visual technologies for inclusion: Speech intelligibility beyond hearing limits"
4. **Brent Edwards** — "Revolutionizing Audiological Care with AI"

## Featured Talks

- **Marina Salorio Corbetto** — "Audiology meets Virtual Reality: The BEARS project"
- **Sheila Templado** — "Auditory Frequency-Following Responses and Cortical Auditory Evoked Potentials Using CLAD"
- **Caleb Rascón** — "Potential Impact of Real-time Auditory Scene Analysis on Hearing Aid Research"
- **Dietmar M. Wohlbauer** — "Assessing neural sensitivity in cochlear implant stimulation"
- **Malcolm Slaney** — "The different ML/AI techniques used to model the auditory system"
- **Fotios Drakopoulos** — "Modelling neural coding in the auditory brain through large-scale electrophysiology"

## Session Topics

### Day 1
- **Spatial Hearing & Listening in Complex Environments** — minimum audible angle, vertical localization, spatial acuity in CI users, speech perception in noise/reverb
- **Cognitive & Neuro-Audiology** — perceptual learning with vocoded speech, tinnitus neuroanatomy, audiovisual speech processing, CAPD assessment
- **Predictive Audiometry & Family-Centred Rehabilitation** — ML-based audiometric classification, Gaussian process regression for audiometry, data-driven phenotypes
- **Special Session: Implementation of AI into Clinical Practice**
- **Innovations in Hearing-Aid Technology** — hearing aid development platforms, dynamic range compression, environmental sound recognition, Clarity Prediction Challenge
- **AI-Powered Tele-Audiology** — automated speech audiometry scoring, smartphone hearing test apps, AI clinical audiometry simulation (AICAS)

### Day 2
- **Computational Models & Signal Processing** — scene recognition with YAMNet+, CARFAC cochlear model, biomimetic CI coding
- **AI-Driven Assessment & Clinical Training** — virtual patient training, language-agnostic speech tests, explainable ML frameworks
- **Cochlear-Implant Signal Processing** — adversarial training for denoising, timbre preservation, channel selection, fNIRS cortical hemodynamics
- **Neuro-Cognitive Markers** — misophonia neural processing, pupil dilation auditory belief updating, cortical benefits of DNN noise management
- **Vestibular & Balance / Middle-Ear Diagnostics** — earable balance assessment, geospatial otitis media mapping, piezoelectric eardrum actuators
- **Computational Modelling of Hearing** — attentional selection limits, computational loudness, auditory evoked potentials, neural health models
- **Next-Generation Digital Audiology** — AI attitudes in audiology students, AI chatbots as hearing health sources, AI misinformation in audiology
- **Special Session: Towards Data Standards in Audiology** — led by Antje Heinrich and Mareike Buhl

## Notable Presentations

- **Robert Eikelboom** — ML study on audiometrically normal clients
- **Mériem Jaïdane** — Gaussian Process Regression for Hughston-Westlake protocol
- **Tom Gajecki** — Adversarial training for speech denoising in CIs
- **Jonathan Vaisberg** — Cortical benefits of DNN-based noise management
- **Annesya Banerjee** — ML models demonstrating attentional selection limits
- **Nicky Chong-White** — Training clinician communication with virtual patients

## Key Extraction

VCCA2025 showed major growth in AI-for-audiology research, with dedicated sessions on clinical AI implementation and data standards. The conference bridged computational modelling, wearable diagnostics (earables), and real-world clinical translation. Panel discussion "Can AI truly transform audiology practice?" highlighted tensions between research promise and clinical reality.
