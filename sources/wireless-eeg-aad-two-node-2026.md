# Wireless EEG Sensor Network for Auditory Attention Decoding

## Summary

Demonstrates auditory attention decoding using a minimal two-node, four-channel wireless EEG sensor network. Provides empirical investigation of the effects of miniaturization on AAD performance, moving toward practical form factors for daily-use neurosteered hearing devices.

## Key Contributions

- Two-node wireless setup vs. traditional high-density scalp EEG
- Only 4 EEG channels — dramatically reduced from 64+ channel lab setups
- Wireless transmission — no tethered cables
- Empirical evaluation of how miniaturization affects decoding accuracy
- Published on bioRxiv February 2026

## Significance

High-density scalp EEG is impractical for daily hearing aid use (bulky, uncomfortable, conspicuous). This work shows that AAD is feasible with minimal, wearable electrode configurations, bringing neurosteered hearing aids closer to consumer form factors.
