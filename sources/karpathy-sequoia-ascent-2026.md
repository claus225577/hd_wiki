# Karpathy — Sequoia AI Ascent 2026 Fireside

## Key Facts

- Fireside chat at Sequoia AI Ascent 2026 with Stephanie Zhan
- Recap published by Karpathy on his blog ~end of April 2026
- Central theme: shift from "vibe coding" to **agentic engineering** (Software 3.0)
- LLMs aren't just speeding up existing work — they enable new categories of products
- New examples discussed: menugen (full LLM-engulfed app), nanochat, autoresearch

## Quote that travelled

> "You can outsource your thinking, but you can't outsource your understanding."

Highlighted by Karpathy as the line that "blew his mind" from the audience reaction. Frames his answer to: *what remains worth learning deeply when intelligence becomes cheap?*

## Software 3.0 Framing

- **Software 1.0:** explicitly written code
- **Software 2.0:** trained neural network weights
- **Software 3.0:** prompting an LLM interpreter; natural language is the programming surface

Implications for engineering culture:
- Agents handle execution (loops, sweeps, scaffolding, lit-review, refactors)
- Human bottleneck shifts to **taste, judgment, definition of what is worth building**
- Engineers who matter most are not the ones writing the least code, but the ones who **understand the most about why it matters and when the agent is wrong**

## Relevance to Hearing + AI

- Hearing aid R&D is a constrained, high-leverage domain for agentic experimentation: bounded search spaces (DSP parameters, fitting protocols), measurable objectives (SII, intelligibility), and many cycles per chip generation.
- Agentic engineering accelerates the *execution* side of hearing AI — ablations, ICASSP-style benchmark sweeps, clinical writeup drafts, regulatory boilerplate.
- The *understanding* side — knowing which audiogram is the wrong one to validate on, why a model fails at 70 dB SPL but not 65, when SII gains come from training-set leakage — remains the human moat.
- Direct line to existing wiki: complements `wiki/concepts/autonomous-research-agents.md` (Karpathy's autoresearch as the execution engine) and `wiki/syntheses/ai-understanding-gap-hearing-industry.md` (the human side).
- Connects to broader question for hearing-care education: what should an audiology PhD or hearing-aid DSP engineer learn deeply *now* — given that agents will increasingly cover the rest?
