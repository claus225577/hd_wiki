# ASHA National Speech-Language-Hearing Month 2026 — Care-Partner Resource Push

## Source
ASHA's official NSLHM 2026 page (May 2026 observance). Public-facing resource hub launched for the May 2026 awareness month.

## What's Notable
The 2026 resource set leans heavily into a population the **hearing-aid industry rarely models in product or in data: care partners**.

Specific resource categories prominent in the 2026 push:
- "Care Partners of Adults" — articles for spouses/partners of adults with hearing loss.
- Adult children of parents with dementia (substantial overlap with hearing-loss/dementia link).
- Families of adults with swallowing and communication disorders.
- Families and caregivers of preschool-age children — tips on screen time and technology.
- A new ASHA–Lantern Texting Program focused on feeding challenges.

The page reads as ASHA explicitly acknowledging that the **dyad** (or family unit), not the individual patient, is the unit of clinical impact.

## Why It Matters for Hearing AI / Data Science
The hearing-aid industry's data and AI architecture is overwhelmingly **n=1** around the wearer:
- Own-voice detectors are trained against one speaker.
- Telemetry attributes events to one device-user.
- Personalization optimizes one user's preferences.
- Speech enhancement targets the wearer's intelligibility — not the partner's experience of being heard, repeated, or relied on for translation.
- Fitting workflows have one slider for one ear pair.

Yet a decade of adherence and outcome data — including cortical-reorganization (Becker et al. 2026), digital-phenotyping (Cejudo et al. 2026), and longitudinal-phenotyping work — converges on the dyad as the unit of adoption. Wear time, satisfaction, and continuation of use all track care-partner involvement more reliably than they track audiogram severity.

## Implications (LinkedIn-Post Argument)
The next interesting hearing-aid feature may not be a bigger model. It may be a **multi-user data schema**:
- Conversation partners as a first-class entity in the device's world model.
- Telemetry of repair requests, repetitions, and breakdowns (which the microphone array already sees).
- Fitting feedback collected from both members of the dyad, not only the wearer.
- Outcome metrics that include the care partner's listening effort, not only the wearer's word recognition.

## Verification Status
ASHA's NSLHM 2026 page does not declare a single "official theme" — care partners are one of several resource topic clusters for the month. The post should frame this as ASHA's *2026 resource focus* rather than the official theme.
