# FDA Jan 2026 Guidance — Low-Risk AI-Enabled Wearables

## Source Metadata
- **Agency:** US FDA
- **Date:** January 2026
- **Vehicle:** Updated guidance reducing oversight of low-risk AI-enabled wearables and wellness devices
- **Surfaced via:** Biometric Update, May 2026 coverage of DoD biometric earwear funding

## What Changed
FDA narrowed the set of AI-enabled wearable/wellness features that require formal Software-as-a-Medical-Device (SaMD) review. Features framed as **wellness** — general fitness, sleep, hydration, stress, non-diagnostic "hearing wellness" — fall outside the active-device pathway. **Hearing aid** functionality (amplification with prescribed targets, fitting via on-device audiometry, OTC HAF certification) remains regulated under the existing 2022 OTC rule and Apple OTC HAF precedent.

## Why It Matters for Hearing Aids
- **Accelerates the OTC + consumer-hearable convergence on paper.** Apple AirPods Pro / AirPods 4 ANC sit on the regulated side; an emerging tier of "hearing wellness" earwear features (Lexie, Eargo wellness telemetry, Bose-licensed hearables, ORKA-style ANC hearables) sits on the deregulated side.
- **Practical consequence:** more unvalidated "hearing wellness" / "tinnitus management" / "auditory training" claims will hit market without 510(k) or De Novo review. Audiologists and clinicians will need to triage what's real vs marketing.
- **Risk surface:** generative SE (per L3-SE / linguistic-hallucination concept) and adaptive on-device personalization fall in a regulatory grey zone — generative speech reconstruction can plausibly be framed as either "noise reduction" (regulated when bundled with HA target) or "audio enhancement" (wellness). The classification will shape liability and reimbursement.
- **Strategic question for OEMs:** does Sonova / Demant / GN / Starkey push for harder regulation to protect the prescription-tier moat, or for lighter regulation to enable hearable product lines? Likely answer is "harder for SE / generative, lighter for telemetry / wellness sensors."
