## Key Facts

- April 2026 paper from University of Michigan Engineering on hardware-software co-design methodologies for running AI efficiently on edge devices.
- Co-design approach: model architecture, quantization strategy, and silicon datapath are designed jointly rather than sequentially, enabling tighter optimization loops.
- Demonstrates significant reduction in energy per inference while preserving accuracy — critical for battery-powered wearables.
- Directly relevant to future hearing aid AI chip design: next-generation devices will require more capable DNNs within strict power budgets (typically <1mW total system power for ITE devices).
- Methods applicable to neuromorphic approaches, mixed-precision inference, and in-memory computing architectures.
- Provides academic framework context for understanding commercial chip developments such as Phonak's DEEPSONIC and Starkey's G3 NPU.
