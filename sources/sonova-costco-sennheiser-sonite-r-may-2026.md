# Sonova Returns to Costco with Sennheiser Sonite R (May 2026)

**Date:** May 11, 2026
**Source:** HearingTracker

## Key Details

- Sonova products are returning to **Costco** via a **pilot trial** with the **Sennheiser Sonite R** — a receiver-in-canal (RIC) hearing aid manufactured by Sennheiser (the consumer-hearing line Sonova licenses).
- Initial deployment in **100+ U.S. Costco warehouses**.
- Marks a reversal of Sonova's well-publicized 2024 pullout, when the company halted Phonak-branded hearing aid sales to Costco and pulled products and brochures.
- Sonite R is positioned at a different price/tier than Phonak's flagship Audéo Sphere Infinio — Costco-channel-appropriate.

## Significance for Hearing AI

- **Retail-data channel re-opens.** Costco's Kirkland Signature audiology operation has historically been one of the largest single-channel sources of fitting and outcome data in the U.S. market. A pilot return — even under the Sennheiser brand — restores Sonova's exposure to that data flow.
- **Brand-stratification strategy.** Sonova now operates a multi-brand retail stack: Phonak (premium, independent network), Unitron (mid-tier, independent), Sennheiser-licensed Sonite R (Costco mass channel), AudioNova (Sonova's own retail consolidation). Each channel produces a distinct fitting-data distribution — relevant for any future centralized ML training pipeline.
- **Costco volume + Sonova R&D = potential dataset asymmetry.** If the pilot scales, Sonova would have the largest hearing-care data footprint of any OEM (R&D depth + Phonak/Unitron + retail consolidation + Costco channel).

## Context

- The earlier 2024 pullout was widely interpreted as a margin/strategy decision rather than a quality issue.
- The Sennheiser consumer-hearing line is licensed/owned by Sonova following the 2022 Sennheiser Consumer division acquisition.
- AudioNova rebrand (May 1, 2026) consolidated Sonova's retail under one banner; the Costco return is a complementary channel, not a substitute.

## Open Questions

- Is the Sonite R built on the DEEPSONIC platform or a lower-cost chipset?
