# Sam's Club Hearing Centers — NationsBenefits Replaces Lucid (May 2026)

## Headline

HearingTracker reports Sam's Club is transitioning operational support of its in-club hearing centers (~450 locations) from **Lucid Hearing** (a WS Audiology subsidiary) to **NationsBenefits** (Plantation, FL — operates NationsHearing for Aetna Medicare Advantage since 2021).

## Key Extraction

- **What changes:** The day-to-day operational stack for Sam's Club Hearing Aid Centers — staffing model, fitting workflow, benefits-administration plumbing — moves to NationsBenefits. Lucid had previously staffed and run the network (~450+ locations, free hearing tests for ages 19+, Lucid prescription HAs $1,299–$5,000/pair, Lucid OTC $200–$850).
- **Strategic context:**
  - WS Audiology loses a major mass-retail footprint at the same moment it is staking its post-Sony OTC strategy on the **Lexie B3 powered by Bose** (launched ~6–7 May 2026; covered in 9 May digest).
  - NationsBenefits already runs NationsHearing for Aetna MA. Folding Sam's Club into the same operator consolidates the Medicare Advantage / OTC benefit-administrator stack *directly into the retail clinic* — reduces friction between MA-funded benefits and in-store fulfillment.
  - This is a **channel-economics shift**, not a product shift. The hearing aids on the shelf may not change immediately; the back-end and the dispensing model do.

## Why This Matters

- **WSA's mass-retail dependency is exposed.** Sony exit + losing Sam's Club operational role in the same quarter means WSA's US OTC pipeline is materially narrower than it was at the start of 2026. Lexie B3's success or failure becomes more load-bearing for WSA's US retail story.
- **Vertical integration of MA benefit administrator + retail clinic** is a model that, if it scales, could pressure Costco/CostcoHearing-style partnerships (Costco's Kirkland Signature is currently sourced from Rexton, also WSA — different channel, but adjacent dynamics).
- **Audiologist-shortage tie-in:** A benefits-administrator-run clinic model probably leans more on technician/specialist staffing than on doctoral-level audiologists, which dovetails with the AAA 2026 panel framing (already in wiki) that the supply constraint is providers, not technology.

## Open Questions

- Timing of transition (gradual vs cut-over)?
- Effect on Lucid's prescription HA business outside Sam's Club (Lucid retains independent retail + LG smart-TV hearing assessment partnership announced earlier).
- Does this signal a wider Walmart/Sam's Club benefits-administrator preference that other retailers (Costco, BJs) might follow?
