# Widex Allure AI RIC (May 20, 2026)

## Summary
On May 20, 2026, Widex (a WS Audiology brand) announced the **Allure AI RIC**, a receiver-in-canal hearing aid built around a dedicated AI co-processor with a **user-toggled "Clarity Boost"** button. Unlike the always-on AI design pattern adopted by Phonak Sphere Infinio, ReSound Vivia, Oticon Zeal/Intent, and Starkey Omega, the Allure AI runs Widex's classical **PureSound** "natural sound" pipeline by default and engages the AI processing path only **on demand**.

Announced alongside the device: **Widex Compass Cloud 2.0**, billed as the world's first cloud-based fitting software, with version-controlled centralized fitting, an Adaptation Manager for patient onboarding, SmartSpeak spoken status feedback, expanded Data Logging, Sound Class Adjustments, and Transfer Settings.

## Headline Facts (as published)
- **Announcement date:** May 20, 2026.
- **Brand:** Widex (WS Audiology).
- **Chip:** Proprietary **W1** with "Precision Hearing Technology"; **dedicated AI co-processor** for audio-domain neural network inference.
- **AI mode:** User-toggled "Clarity Boost" — engaged on demand via button press. Default operating mode is Widex's classical **PureSound** pipeline with **ZeroDelay**.
- **Claimed performance:** Up to **6 dB higher output SNR vs competitor AI hearing aids** when Clarity Boost is on.
- **Battery:** **32 hours total**, including **up to 6 hours of AI-on use or streaming**.
- **Feature set:** Speech Enhancer Pro, Enhanced Sound Classifier, Adaptive Dynamic Feedback Controller, Allure PureSound with ZeroDelay, Dedicated Streaming Compressor.
- **Connectivity:** Made for iPhone (MFi), ASHA, **LE Audio**, **telecoil**, hands-free.
- **Charging:** Standard, Charge & Clean, **Qi-enabled portable**.
- **Companion fitting platform:** **Widex Compass Cloud 2.0** — features Adaptation Manager (patient onboarding), SmartSpeak (spoken status), expanded Data Logging, Sound Class Adjustments, Transfer Settings; **99.9%+ uptime**; **4/5 HCP satisfaction** in pre-launch testing.
- **Market launch:** **5 markets June 1, 2026**; remaining markets including **US November 1, 2026**.

## Executive Quote
> "We've taken a different approach, preserving natural sound as the foundation and introducing AI processing only when it adds value."
> — Ngozi Amobi, head of Widex

## Strategic / Data-Science Significance
- **Counter-positioning** against the always-on AI race led by Phonak Sphere Infinio, ReSound Vivia, Oticon Zeal/Intent, and Starkey Omega.
- A user-controlled AI button is a **labeled training signal that always-on systems cannot produce**: every button press is a "this scene warranted AI" label tied to the scene-classifier state, acoustic features, time, and streaming source. This is structurally novel telemetry.
- Pairs naturally with **Compass Cloud 2.0** as a centralized fitting + version control + retraining substrate. The closed loop is materializing at the **platform layer**, not the chip layer.
- Distinct from prior WSA initiatives — the **Sound Preference Tool** (Apr 2026, preference phenotyping at fitting time) and the **Korhonen et al. Widex Allure non-AI study** (Apr 2026, natural processing outperforming DNN). The Allure AI RIC is a **separate product variant** — the "AI" version with the dedicated co-processor — and represents WSA leaning into a **hybrid story** rather than picking a side in the natural-vs-DNN debate.

## Limits / Open Questions
- The "up to 6 dB higher output SNR vs competitor AI hearing aids" claim is manufacturer-published. Independent replication pending.
- Real-world distribution of Clarity Boost engagement (how often, in what scenes, by which demographic) is unmeasured externally as of launch.
- Whether WSA will surface aggregated button-press telemetry into Compass Cloud 2.0 as a personalization/retraining signal has not been publicly confirmed.
