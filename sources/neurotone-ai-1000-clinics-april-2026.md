# Neurotone AI — 1,000 Clinic Partners Milestone

## Key Facts

- **Neurotone AI** surpassed **1,000 clinic partners** — significant adoption milestone for AI-powered aural rehabilitation
- **Dr. Brian Taylor** joined as **VP Clinical Research** — formerly of Signia (WS Audiology); well-known clinical audiology thought leader
- AI-powered aural rehabilitation becoming a **standard of care** — no longer experimental or supplementary
- Platform uses AI to deliver personalized auditory training exercises for hearing aid users

## Significance

- **1,000 clinics** represents meaningful market penetration — aural rehabilitation has traditionally been underutilized due to time constraints and lack of reimbursement
- **Brian Taylor hire** is a notable talent move: a respected clinical voice moving from a Big 6 manufacturer (Signia/WSA) to an AI startup signals the legitimacy of AI aural rehab
- Positioning aural rehab as "standard of care" — if achieved, this creates a new recurring revenue stream alongside hearing aid sales
- Demonstrates that AI in audiology extends beyond the hearing aid device itself — platform-level AI services for clinics

## Relevance to Hearing + AI

- **AI aural rehabilitation** is a distinct category from hearing aid DNN processing — it uses AI for training the listener (brain), not processing the sound (device)
- Addresses the "beyond the hearing aid" opportunity: most hearing AI focuses on device-level processing; Neurotone targets post-fitting outcomes
- **1,000-clinic scale** generates significant outcome data that could validate (or refute) AI aural rehab efficacy at population scale
- Brian Taylor's clinical research background suggests Neurotone is building an evidence base for the approach
- **Convergence with hearing aid AI:** As hearing aids get smarter at processing sound, parallel improvement in listener training could be multiplicative — better sound processing + better-trained listener = compounding benefit
