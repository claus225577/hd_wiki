# IHS Better Hearing Month 2026

## Summary
The **International Hearing Society (IHS)** is using May 2026 (Better Hearing Month) to surface recent research framing untreated hearing loss as a **causal** contributor to cognitive decline, not merely correlated. The campaign emphasizes proactive hearing care.

## Key Claim
- A **2026 study** is cited as supporting a *causal* relationship between hearing loss and cognitive decline. (Note: the specific study is not named in the IHS landing page; verify the underlying source before citing — the ACHIEVE trial 2025 readout and follow-on analyses are the most likely referents.)

## Strategic Read
- "Causal" framing is the strongest evidence claim yet from industry-aligned bodies and aligns with the AAA 2026 panel's push to reframe hearing loss as a core health issue.
- If the underlying study is robust, this materially strengthens the case for **insurance coverage** and **earlier intervention** — both have downstream implications for AI-driven screening tools (e.g., phone-based audiometry, EHR triggers).
- Public-awareness campaigns in May historically drive seasonal uplift in hearing-care visits — operationally useful timing for clinics to roll out AI-assisted triage tools.

## Caveats
- "Causal" is a strong claim that requires careful reading. Most existing evidence (Lancet Commission updates, ACHIEVE) shows hearing-aid use *slows* cognitive decline in at-risk populations rather than proving full causation in the general population.
