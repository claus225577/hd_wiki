# Phonak DNN Noise Reduction Clinical Trial

## Key Extraction

- **Registry:** ClinicalTrials.gov, NCT07526428
- **Type:** Interventional study
- **Focus:** DNN-based noise reduction for adults with moderate-to-severe hearing loss
- **Sponsor:** Phonak (Sonova)
- **Registration date:** April 14, 2026
- **Significance:**
  - First registered clinical trial specifically evaluating DNN noise reduction in hearing aids for moderate-to-severe loss (most DNN hearing aid studies focus on mild-to-moderate)
  - Signals Sonova's push to generate clinical evidence for DEEPSONIC platform efficacy
  - Moderate-to-severe population is underrepresented in DNN hearing aid research; these users have different SNR needs
  - Interventional design (not observational) suggests controlled comparison against a baseline (likely classical DSP or previous-gen Phonak)
  - Clinical trial registration indicates Sonova is building an evidence base that could support regulatory claims, insurance reimbursement arguments, and clinical guideline inclusion
