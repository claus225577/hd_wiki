# Acousia Therapeutics — Bimokalner Phase 2a Positive (Jun 18, 2026)

## Announcement

**Acousia Therapeutics GmbH** (Tübingen, Germany) reported positive top-line results from its **Phase 2a ProHear Study** of **ACOU085 / Bimokalner** on **18 June 2026** via Hearing Review. Trial site: Tübingen University Medical Center.

## Drug

- **ACOU085 (Bimokalner)** is a small-molecule **Kv7.4 (KCNQ4) potassium-channel modulator**.
- Mechanism: activates the Kv7.4 channel in **outer hair cells (OHCs)** of the cochlea, the same channel whose loss-of-function mutations cause DFNA2 progressive hearing loss in humans. Pharmacological activation is hypothesised to protect OHCs against acquired insults (ototoxic chemotherapy, noise).
- **Formulation:** transtympanic slow-release gel — single application delivered through the tympanic membrane into the middle ear, with diffusion across the round window membrane into the cochlea. Localised, not systemic. Sits in the same precision-inner-ear-delivery class as Spiral Therapeutics' platform but for a small-molecule drug rather than a steroid/growth-factor adjunct.

## Trial Design

- **Phase 2a ProHear Study** — exploratory, placebo-controlled, **split-body design** (one ear treated with ACOU085 gel, contralateral ear placebo).
- **Population:** testicular cancer patients receiving **cisplatin 300 mg/m² over 3 cycles** — a chemotherapy regimen with high and quantifiable ototoxicity risk, making it a near-ideal clean-signal substrate for an otoprotection trial.
- **Primary outcome:** prevention of pure-tone-average (PTA) threshold shifts vs placebo at audiometric frequencies including the extended high-frequency (EHF) range.

## Results

- **>90% of participants developed ototoxicity in at least one ear** — confirming the high event rate expected for the cisplatin 300 mg/m² regimen and validating the within-subject design (treatment effect against a contralateral control in the same patient).
- Ototoxicity manifested predominantly in the **extended high-frequency range (10–16 kHz)**, mild-to-moderate.
- **The treated ear showed clinically meaningful prevention of PTA threshold shifts vs placebo.**
- Read-out is consistent with the mechanistic hypothesis (Kv7.4 activation protects OHCs from cisplatin-induced apoptosis); EHF-range signal is exactly where cisplatin damage manifests first.

## Next Steps

- Collaboration / partnership discussions scheduled at the **BIO International Convention**, San Diego, **22–25 June 2026**.
- Trial outcome positions Acousia as the most advanced clinical-stage programme targeting OHC-side pharmacological otoprotection.

## Why This Matters for Hearing AI / Devices

1. **Otoprotection as an upstream layer to the HA market.** If pharmacological prevention of OHC loss becomes a standard adjunct to ototoxic chemotherapy (cisplatin is used in ~1M oncology patients annually worldwide), the candidate population for hearing aids in the 5–15-year-out chemotherapy-survivor cohort shrinks at the EHF / mild-to-moderate end of the audiogram — precisely the demographic OTC and emerging AI-hearable products are growing into. This is a long-tail upstream substitution effect rather than a near-term threat.
2. **The EHF 10–16 kHz target axis is the same axis the subjective-objective gap and hidden HL lines of work have called out as the under-instrumented frequency range.** Acousia's trial succeeded by *measuring* in EHF — which is also what Dillon (VCCA 2026 keynote), Knipper (Tübingen public statement, May 2026), and the Apple Hearing Study's "16% of clinically normal report fair/poor hearing" have all named as the audiometric blind spot. A successful otoprotection drug operationalises EHF audiometry as a billable, FDA-relevant outcome — pulling the whole audiology field's measurement substrate one frequency band higher.
3. **Tübingen is becoming an OHC-biology cluster.** Knipper's hidden-HL lab and Acousia (founded out of the Tübingen ENT department) sit in the same city. The clinical-trial site is the Tübingen University Medical Center. This is the same kind of geographic concentration the field saw earlier with Oldenburg (HA signal processing) and Cambridge (CI signal processing).
4. **The drug + device + ML stack is being assembled in public.** With the Otarmeni / Harvard OTOF gene-therapy approvals at one end (genetic SNHL) and Acousia's OHC-pharmacology at the other (acquired ototoxic SNHL), and with hidden-HL diagnostics maturing in parallel at VCCA 2026, the field now has at least four active intervention layers — gene therapy, drug, device, ML/sensor. The data substrate connecting them (longitudinal phenotyping across EHF audiometry + speech-in-noise + telemetry) is the most underbuilt layer.
