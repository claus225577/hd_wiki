# NYU Langone — AI in Cochlear-Implant Programming

## Summary
NYU Langone is among a small group of US sites running a clinical trial that uses **AI to support cochlear-implant programming** — the per-electrode mapping process that translates acoustic input to electrical stimulation patterns. Programming has remained largely manual, audiologist-driven, and inconsistent across centers for nearly three decades.

## Trial Design (as described)
- For **newly implanted patients**: measure speech-recognition improvements over 6 months under AI-driven mapping.
- For **existing CI users**: assess whether AI-driven *re-programming* improves outcomes vs. status-quo programming.

## Why It Matters
- CI programming is a textbook case of **subjective, expert-bottlenecked clinical workflow** — exactly the kind of place where ML can add objectivity and scale.
- A successful trial would directly attack the audiologist-shortage problem: AI-assisted mapping reduces per-patient programming time and lowers the experience floor required to deliver good outcomes.
- Aligned with the broader trend (Northwestern's pediatric-language-prediction model, end-to-end audiovisual CI sound coding) toward ML touching every step of the CI patient journey.

## Open Questions
- What model class is being used (supervised regression on programming parameters? RL on speech-recognition reward?).
- Generalization across CI manufacturers (Cochlear / Advanced Bionics / MED-EL) — each has a different electrode array and a different mapping parameter space.
- Regulatory pathway: clinical-decision-support AI vs. SaMD.
