# World Congress of Audiology 2026 — Seoul

## Key Facts

- **Conference:** World Congress of Audiology (WCA) 2026
- **Organizer:** International Society of Audiology (ISA)
- **Dates:** May 24–27, 2026
- **Location:** Seoul, Republic of Korea
- **Cadence:** ISA's flagship biennial event, rotating across continents

## Why This Is Notable

WCA is the most genuinely global event on the audiology calendar — non-Western health-system priorities (universal newborn hearing screening, low-cost OTC adoption in LMIC contexts, mobile audiometry on smartphones) typically get more airtime here than at AAA / ASHA. Worth tracking the program for:

- AI / teleaudiology sessions (likely heavy given Korean industrial focus on consumer audio + telehealth)
- Hearing-aid affordability and access from a non-U.S. perspective (relevant to the WHO 80% access-gap framing)
- Sessions on Asian-market hearing aid OEMs and supply-chain dynamics

## Program at a Glance (confirmed as of May 13, 2026)

- **Theme:** "Broadening the Horizon of Audiology"
- **Scale:** 60 planned sessions, 10 hands-on workshops, 18 special sessions, 32 oral presentation sessions, plus innovation-focused sessions and posters
- **Plenary lectures with explicit AI / new-tech framing:**
  - "Future Perspectives in Audiology and AI, New Technologies"
  - "When Audiology Meets Auditory Neuroscience: Bridging Clinical Practice and Brain Science"
  - "Audiology in Low Resource Regions: Diagnostics and Rehabilitation"

## Computational Audiology Network (CAN) Contributions

The Computational Audiology Network is a multi-session presence at WCA 2026 — a meaningful escalation from prior congresses where computational topics were largely confined to individual oral presentations:

- **Round Table:** "Future Perspectives in Audiology and AI, New Technologies" — moderated by Nicky Chong-White
- **Workshop:** "The Force Awakens: Robots, Avatars, and ASR in Clinical Audiology" — Gloria Araiza-Illan and Jan-Willem Wasmann
- **Planned Session:** "Future e-Health — AI for the Ear and Beyond" — featuring:
  - AI Virtual Patients for Audiology Training
  - Piloting AI-Assisted Diagnosis of Childhood Ear Disease
  - VR-Based Fine-Tuning of Hearing Device Settings

## Significance for Hearing Aid AI

- **Computational audiology graduates from sidecar to track.** Three formal CAN slots at a flagship global congress, alongside AI-framed plenaries, is structurally different from individual papers slotted into general sessions at AAA / ASHA in prior years.
- **Hiring-pipeline implication.** Audiology graduates landing at Sonova, Demant, GN, Starkey, and WSA in 2027–2028 will arrive with formal ML / AI exposure that wasn't in their curriculum three years ago. The "data-fluent audiologist" is moving from unicorn job description to new baseline.
- **Validation-loop compression.** More clinicians who can read a confusion matrix, challenge a held-out set, and design protocols against ML-style evaluation = fewer translation steps between R&D and clinic.
- **Asian-market exposure.** Korean-market positioning increases the chance of seeing presentations from local OEMs and OTC players that don't typically headline AAA / Demant-region conferences.
