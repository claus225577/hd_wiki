# Trihear ClearTV Series Launch

## Bibliographic
- **Company:** Trihear
- **Product line:** ClearTV (Bluetooth) and ClearTV ASHA
- **Launch:** 4 Jun 2026
- **URL (coverage):** https://hearinghealthmatters.org/hearing-technologies/2026/cleartv-hearing-aids-app-free/
- **URL (product):** https://trihear.com/products/trihear-cleartv

## What
ClearTV is a wireless TV streamer that delivers low-latency stereo TV audio directly to hearing aids without requiring a smartphone or companion app. Two SKUs:
- **ClearTV** ($99) — Bluetooth, compatible with select Phonak and Unitron hearing aids.
- **ClearTV ASHA** ($119) — Audio Streaming for Hearing Aids (ASHA) protocol, compatible with select Oticon, Signia, Widex, Starkey, ReSound, Jabra, and Philips models.

Specs:
- Integrated LCD with on-device setup guidance.
- Built-in rechargeable battery, ~12 hours per charge.
- Optical (TOSLINK) and 3.5 mm analog inputs.
- Plug-and-play installation.

## Why It Matters
ClearTV is a deliberate counter-position to the app-only ecosystem the OEMs have been pushing for the better part of a decade. Two structural points:
1. **UX-simplification as a wedge product category.** A non-trivial share of HA users are older adults who never installed or stopped using the OEM companion apps. Trihear has identified that share as an addressable accessory market and is monetizing it at ~$99-119 per unit.
2. **ASHA as a near-universal accessory protocol.** ASHA is the de facto cross-OEM streaming standard for the long tail of HA brands; Phonak/Unitron stay on Bluetooth Classic. ClearTV bridging both with separate SKUs is a useful data point on what shipping into the fragmented HA-accessory market actually requires.

Worth tracking alongside the broader Auracast venue rollout: ClearTV is the home-TV slice of the same "drop the app, stream from the source" UX pattern that Auracast is going to commoditize in public venues over the next two years.

## Context
- Coincides with renewed focus on the audiologist time crisis (Auditdata June 2026 survey) — accessories with zero-touch UX reduce post-fitting support burden.
- Sits adjacent to the broader "user-controlled on-demand AI" wave — the simplification thesis on accessories complements the AI-personalization thesis on the HA itself.
