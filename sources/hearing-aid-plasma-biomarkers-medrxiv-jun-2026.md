# Hearing Aids and Plasma Biomarkers of Alzheimer's Disease

## Citation
*The Effect of Treating Hearing Loss with Hearing Aids on Plasma Biomarkers of Alzheimer's Disease and Related Dementias.* medRxiv preprint (10.1101/2025.11.19.25340558), surfaced via PubMed indexing on 27 Jun 2026.

## Key Findings
- **Estimated 7-year dementia risk ~33% lower** in participants who received a new hearing-aid prescription by the start of follow-up vs. those who did not.
- **Longitudinal cognitive scores moved only modestly** — the dementia-risk effect is meaningfully larger than the cognitive-trajectory effect, mirroring the pattern previously seen in ACHIEVE.
- **Plasma biomarker readouts** (Aβ42/40 ratio, p-tau181/p-tau217, NfL, GFAP — exact panel TBD on full read) layered on top of the cognitive endpoints. This is the contribution that distinguishes the preprint from prior cohort analyses.

## Why It Matters for Hearing AI
- **Mechanistic vs. behavioural endpoints.** A divergence between biomarker change and cognitive trajectory speaks directly to the open question of whether hearing-aid use slows underlying neurodegenerative pathology or simply preserves cognitive *test performance* via better encoding of stimuli.
- **Adds biological plausibility** to the public-health argument for hearing-aid uptake and reimbursement.
- **Feeds the case for early intervention** — the larger the lead time between hearing-aid fitting and biomarker stabilisation, the more important detection (audiometry, OAE screening, AI-based hidden-loss detection) becomes.
- **Connects to FDA Breakthrough designation pipeline** for any AI-enabled hearing aid claiming a cognitive or dementia-prevention benefit, since plasma biomarkers are becoming the dominant supporting endpoint.

## Open Questions / Things to Watch
- Which plasma biomarkers move and which don't? An NfL effect (general neurodegeneration) would tell a different story than an Aβ effect (AD-specific).
- How does the effect size compare to the ARIC-NCS cross-sectional plasma-biomarker findings (which suggested HL associates with broader neurodegeneration markers, not AD-specific ones)?
- Confounding by hearing-aid-user selection effects (already a known limitation of the ACHIEVE-adjacent observational arm).
