# WS Audiology Sound Preference Program

## Key Facts
- WS Audiology launched a free **Sound Preference Program** and interactive **Sound Preference Tool**
- Research shows **~40% of users** have strong, consistent preferences for either "natural sound" or "enhanced sound"
- The tool uses **randomized audio comparisons** to identify preferences
- Does not require patients to describe what they hear in words — overcomes the vocabulary barrier in hearing care

## How It Works
- Patients listen to randomized audio comparisons
- The tool classifies preference as "natural sound" (minimal processing, transparency) or "enhanced sound" (more aggressive processing, clarity optimization)
- Results are consistent across sessions for ~40% of users — indicating a stable, measurable trait
- The remaining ~60% show mixed or context-dependent preferences

## Significance
- **Structured data collection framework for personalization** — converts subjective experience into quantifiable preference data
- Reframes the fitting conversation from "what do you hear?" (subjective, vocabulary-dependent) to measurable preference classification
- Creates a standardized input for personalization algorithms
- Aligns with WSA's dual-brand strategy: Widex (known for natural sound quality) vs. Signia (known for enhanced processing/clarity)
- The 40% figure establishes that sound preference is a real, measurable individual trait — not just noise in fitting data
- Potential foundation for ML-driven fitting: preference classification as a feature input for automated gain prescription

## Connection to Data Flywheel
- Free tool means broad adoption potential across WSA's customer base
- Each use generates preference data that could feed back into product R&D
- If integrated with Signia Assistant (cloud DNN), could enable preference-aware personalization at scale
