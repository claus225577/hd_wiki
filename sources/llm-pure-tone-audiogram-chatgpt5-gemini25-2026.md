# Diagnostic Interpretation of Pure Tone Audiograms by Multimodal LLMs

## Key Extractions

### Setup
- Comparative study of two multimodal LLMs — **ChatGPT-5.0 Plus** and **Gemini 2.5** — on the task of interpreting pure-tone audiograms presented as images.
- Evaluation framed around diagnostic accuracy and consistency for use as a screening adjunct.

### Results
- **ChatGPT-5.0 Plus outperformed Gemini 2.5** in both accuracy and consistency on PTA interpretation.
- Authors stop short of endorsing either model as a standalone diagnostic tool.
- Recommended use case: adjunct in **primary care and telehealth screening workflows**, with audiologist review for any clinically meaningful classification.

### Caveats
- Multimodal LLM performance on charts/images remains brittle to formatting, axis conventions, and small-image artifacts.
- No public benchmark for audiogram interpretation exists yet — results are dataset-specific.

## Relevance
- Adds a 2026 data point to the steady stream of "can LLMs do audiology screening?" papers.
- Reinforces the emerging consensus: frontier multimodal LLMs are crossing the bar for triage but not for diagnosis.
- Useful for any tool design where an LLM helps a non-specialist (PCP, OTC retailer, school nurse) interpret a basic audiogram before a referral.
