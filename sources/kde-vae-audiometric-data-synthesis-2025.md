# Synthesizing Audiometric Data with KDE and VAE

## Summary

Develops and validates two complementary approaches for synthesizing audiometric data — Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) and Variational Autoencoders (VAE). Uses NHANES dataset comprising 36,676 participants. Published in Applied Sciences, 2025.

## Key Contributions

- Two data synthesis methods: statistical (KDE) and deep generative (VAE)
- Trained on NHANES — 36,676 participants (large, representative US population data)
- Enables generation of synthetic audiometric data for ML training without privacy concerns
- Validated that synthetic data preserves statistical properties of real data

## Significance

Audiometric data is sensitive health information with privacy constraints. Synthetic data generation enables ML model training, benchmarking, and research sharing without exposing patient data. The comparison of KDE (simple) vs. VAE (complex) helps researchers choose the right approach for their needs.
