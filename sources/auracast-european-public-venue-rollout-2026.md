# Auracast Public Venue Rollout Accelerating

## Key Facts
- Bluetooth SIG reports accelerating Auracast installations across European public venues
- **European airports** piloting Auracast broadcast audio for gate announcements, boarding calls
- **Transit systems** deploying Auracast for station announcements and wayfinding
- **NHS outpatient clinics** in the UK piloting Auracast for patient communication
- Deployment timeline: 2026-2027 for pilot installations

## Significance for Hearing AI
- **NHS adoption** is a major signal: public health system endorsement validates Auracast as an accessibility standard, not just a consumer convenience
- Airport and transit deployments are high-impact: these are notoriously difficult listening environments for hearing aid users (PA systems, background noise, distance from announcer)
- Europe leading adoption likely reflects stronger accessibility regulations (European Accessibility Act 2025)
- Moves Auracast from "UK theater pilots" (April 2026) to systematic public infrastructure deployment
- **Government/NHS funding** could solve the "who pays for infrastructure?" question that has slowed adoption

## Context
- Builds on UK theater Auracast deployments (Everyman Theatre, Contact Manchester — also April 2026)
- Three of six major HA manufacturers now ship Auracast-capable devices
- Venue infrastructure has been the bottleneck — devices were ready before venues
