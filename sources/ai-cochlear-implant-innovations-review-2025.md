## Key Facts

- Review covers five major AI application domains in cochlear implants: speech enhancement, automated fitting/mapping, predictive modeling of outcomes, rehabilitation support, and AI-assisted surgical planning and guidance.
- Speech enhancement algorithms improve CI input signal quality before electrode stimulation, compensating for limited frequency resolution of implant arrays.
- Automated fitting systems use ML to reduce the number of clinic visits required for device programming, a significant access and cost benefit.
- Predictive models identify candidates likely to achieve good speech outcomes, enabling more informed pre-surgical counseling.
- AI-assisted surgery applications include electrode insertion guidance and intraoperative monitoring to minimize cochlear trauma.
- Key challenges identified: computational constraints of implant hardware, heterogeneity of patient data across CI manufacturers, and limited large-scale clinical validation studies.
- Authors call for standardized datasets and cross-manufacturer collaboration to accelerate evidence-based AI adoption in CI care.
