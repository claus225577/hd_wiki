# VCCA 2026 — Main Programme (June 25–26, 2026)

The 7th Virtual Conference on Computational Audiology ran fully virtual, hosted from Spain, free to attend, with talks archived on YouTube. Pre-conference workshops (Jun 18–19) are documented separately in [[vcca2026-pre-conference-workshops-jun-19-2026]]; the main two-day programme overview is in [[vcca2026-conference]]. This source captures the specific keynotes, invited talks, and the two ML/HA-innovation sessions in detail as released on the main programme page.

## Keynote Lectures

### Day 1 (Jun 25)
- **Prof. Harvey Dillon (Macquarie University)** — "Separating the Causes of Listening Difficulties when Thresholds are Normal: Concepts and Data." Direct hit on the suprathreshold / normal-audiogram-but-impaired phenotype — the same construct that surfaces in the Apple Hearing Study 16% finding (subjective-objective gap), in Knipper's hidden-hearing-loss public statement, and in the Session 3.A "Hidden Hearing Loss and Subclinical Auditory Disorders" track Joaquin Valderrama chairs.
- **Prof. Barbara Shinn-Cunningham** — "Hijacking and helping auditory attention: How interruptions and prediction shape everyday communication." Auditory-attention framing of everyday communication; pairs with the AAD / FAConformer / closed-loop ECoG line of work.

### Day 2 (Jun 26)
- **Prof. Linda J. Hood** — "Characterizing auditory physiologic responses in pediatric populations at risk." Pediatric audiology + objective-electrophysiology angle; complements the cross-lifespan / brain-aligned-foundation-models direction.
- **Prof. Enrique A. Lopez-Poveda (Universidad de Salamanca)** — "Computational Auditory Models in the Service of Improving Audiology." The clearest single naming on the programme of the "computational model as substrate, not sidecar" thesis — sits adjacent to Saddler's task-optimized-DNN-as-auditory-model program and Banerjee's auditory-periphery pre-conference workshop.

## Invited Speakers

- **A/Prof. Andrew Dimitrijevic (University of Toronto)** — "A Novel Gamified Neurofeedback System for Enhancing Attention in Naturalistic Listening Environments." Gamified neurofeedback + naturalistic listening — closed-loop attention training on the rehab side of the AAD pipeline.
- **Prof. Sharon Kujawa (Harvard Medical School)** — "Hidden Hearing Loss: From Animal Models to Human Translation." Kujawa's lab is the originator of the synaptopathy / hidden-HL programme (Kujawa & Liberman 2009 J Neurosci); having the founder of the basic-science framework as a keynote-tier invited speaker alongside Dillon's clinical talk is the strongest single signal at VCCA 2026 that hidden HL has graduated from animal-model curiosity to a clinical phenotype the audiology field treats as a first-class outcome.
- **Prof. Dr.-Ing. Waldo Nogueira (Hannover Medical School)** — "Extracochlear Electric–Acoustic Interaction as the Basis for a Novel Hearing Device." A new device class sitting between hearing aid and cochlear implant; novel EAS concept that doesn't require electrode insertion into the cochlea.
- **Prof. Anu Sharma (University of Colorado Boulder)** — "The Changing Brain: Cross-Modal Neuroplasticity in Hearing loss." Cross-modal plasticity as a measurable consequence of untreated hearing loss — supplies neural-side evidence for the dementia / cortical-reorganization pages.

## Session 4.A — Machine Learning & AI for Hearing Science

**Chair:** Prof. Dr. Tobias Goehring (Cambridge).

**Featured talks:**
- **Mark Saddler (DTU Hearing Systems)** — "Perceptual Sensitivity is Explained by Optimization for Ecological Hearing Tasks." Direct extension of the Saddler & McDermott program: task-optimized DNNs trained on biophysically faithful cochlear simulators reproduce human perceptual sensitivity when, and only when, optimized on ecologically realistic tasks. The strongest single hypothesis-grade statement on programme.
- **Frank Betances Reinoso** — "VIA+: A Gamified Digital Ecosystem for the Integrated Phenotyping of Auditory and Cognitive Development." Gamified longitudinal phenotyping platform integrating auditory and cognitive development — pediatric / school-age substrate for the digital-phenotyping line.

**Podium presentations:**
- **Xavier Pérez Ruiz** — DNN-based cochlear-implant sound coding.
- **Joanna Luberadzka** — AI voice agents for speech assessment.
- **Tianyi Liu** — Remote CI programming.
- **Miguel Temboury** — Physics-informed ML for auditory potentials.
- **Thomas Schwarz** — Mobile loudness scaling.

**Poster (called out):**
- **Matthias Inghels** — "Computational Closed-Loop Methods to Compensate for Standard and Hidden Hearing Losses." Names hidden HL directly inside the ML-for-HA poster track — closes the loop between the Dillon/Kujawa diagnostic side of the programme and the algorithm-side compensation side.

## Session 5.B — Hearing-Aid Innovation & Performance Assessment

**Chair:** Dr. Raul Sanchez Lopez.

**Featured talks:**
- **Andrew Sabin** — "A Large-Scale Database and Predictive Model of Listener-Rated Ease of Speech Understanding in Commercial Hearing Aids." Population-scale, commercially-fitted-aid dataset with listener-rated ease-of-understanding labels and a predictive model trained on it. Closest analogue today to "the Clarity Challenge for shipping products" — operationalises ease-of-understanding (not just intelligibility) as a real-world outcome a model can learn.
- **Julie Van Heghe** — "Neural Response-Driven Optimization of Cochlear Implant Stimulation Using Differentiable Auditory Models." Differentiable auditory model in the optimization loop for CI stimulation — direct hit on the DAL / differentiable-cochlea / clinical-ML-convergence thread; first CI-side use of that operator family on the programme.

## Key Extraction

Three structural reads on this programme:

1. **Hidden HL is no longer a sub-community concern.** Two keynotes (Dillon, Kujawa) plus a dedicated session 3.A plus an explicit poster (Inghels) plus the closing "computational closed-loop methods to compensate for standard and hidden hearing losses" framing all hit the same phenotype. The Hidden HL track at VCCA 2026 is the strongest endorsement to date that the audiology community treats suprathreshold deficits as a first-class outcome class, not an oddity.
2. **The differentiable / task-optimized substrate is graduating from one lab to a programmed paradigm.** Saddler's "Perceptual Sensitivity is Explained by Optimization for Ecological Hearing Tasks" pairs with Van Heghe's "Neural Response-Driven Optimization of CI Stimulation Using Differentiable Auditory Models" and with Lopez-Poveda's keynote naming computational auditory models as service infrastructure for audiology. Three independent groups on the same programme using the same operator family across periphery, HA, and CI.
3. **The clinical evaluation stack is widening past intelligibility.** Sabin's commercial-HA database is labelled with listener-rated ease-of-understanding, not WER. That's the operational substrate for the FUEL-listening-effort / communication-accessibility metric layer Pichora-Fuller's WCA 2026 keynote argued the field has not built — but starting to.
