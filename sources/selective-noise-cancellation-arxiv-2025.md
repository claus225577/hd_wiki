## Key Facts

- Convolutional Recurrent Networks (CRNs) identified as optimal architecture for hearing aid applications due to their balance of performance and computational efficiency.
- Transformer and attention-based models enable context-aware selective noise cancellation, capable of differentiating a target speaker from competing talkers and background noise.
- Ultra-low latency of 1ms achieved — meeting the strict real-time processing requirements of hearing aids (typically <10ms end-to-end).
- Selective cancellation approach preserves desired sounds (speech, alarms, music) while suppressing unwanted noise, unlike blanket noise suppression.
- Review covers tradeoffs between model complexity, power consumption, and hearing aid battery life for on-device deployment.
