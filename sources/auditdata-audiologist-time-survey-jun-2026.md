# Auditdata Launches US Audiologist Time-Crisis Survey (4 Jun 2026)

## What

Auditdata launched a US-wide industry survey on **4 June 2026** targeting clinic owners, practice managers, and audiologists. Goal: quantify where clinician time is being lost in US hearing care clinics and what drives burnout. A five-minute instrument; aggregated results to be published as an **industry benchmark later in 2026**.

## Survey Focus

The instrument asks respondents to rate:

- **Severity of staffing pressure** in their organization.
- **Biggest drains on clinician time**, with named examples:
  - TPA portals — TruHearing, UnitedHealthcare Hearing.
  - Prior-authorization workflows.
  - Documentation across disconnected systems.
  - Inconsistent workflows.
- **What would most help close the gap** — open-ended.

## Baseline Cited

- **~48M Americans live with hearing loss.**
- **~75% of US counties** face audiology staffing shortages.
- Documentation, inconsistent workflows, disconnected systems, and TPA paperwork can consume **up to 1 hour per clinician per day**.

## Why It Matters

- **First explicit quantitative industry instrument on administrative-burden time loss.** The audiologist-workforce-shortage narrative has been carried for years by ASHA workforce surveys and BLS projections. This is the first US industry survey specifically targeting the *administrative-time-loss* component — i.e., the mechanism through which back-office AI can buy the field time.
- **Auditdata is a practice-management software vendor.** They have product incentive to frame admin burden as solvable by software. But the survey is open, the instrument is straightforward, and the aggregated benchmark — if published transparently — becomes a primary data source for the back-office AI-leverage thesis (cf. AAA 2026 leadership panel).
- **TPA-portal explicit naming is new.** Public industry discourse has not previously named TruHearing and UHC Hearing as the chief time-cost drivers. Worth tracking whether the published benchmark confirms or revises this.
