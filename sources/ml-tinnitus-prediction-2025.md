## Key Facts

- ML models demonstrated ability to predict tinnitus presence from audiological and patient history data, outperforming traditional clinical heuristics.
- Models can differentiate noise-induced hearing loss (NIHL) from age-related hearing loss (ARHL/presbycusis) — a distinction that is clinically important for treatment planning and medico-legal purposes but historically difficult to make objectively.
- Improved diagnostic accuracy over traditional audiometric pattern analysis alone.
- Features used likely include audiogram shape, notch characteristics (particularly at 4kHz for NIHL), patient demographics, and noise exposure history.
- Implications for occupational health, workers' compensation, and targeted tinnitus intervention planning.
- Demonstrates ML utility in differential diagnosis within audiology beyond pure hearing aid fitting applications.
