# Brian Taylor — Rationale for the Move to Neurotone AI (April 2026)

## Key Extraction

- **Move:** Brian Taylor, longtime audiologist (most recently Senior Director of Audiology at Signia / WS Audiology, ~3 decades in audiology) joined Neurotone AI as **VP Clinical Research and Professional Relations**.
- **Why he moved:** Frames device-side hearing care as having a "missing piece" — what happens *after* fitting. Hardware advances ≠ communication outcomes; better audibility on day one doesn't automatically translate to less listening fatigue or more confidence in real-world conversation.
- **Quote:** "Hearing aids restore access to sound, but rehab can help people get a lot more benefit from that access."
- **Neurotone AI's product:** Smartphone-based AI-personalized auditory training — modules covering speech-in-noise, rapid speech, working memory, and missing-word exercises, with patients working at their own pace on areas most relevant to them.

## Significance / Why This Matters

1. **Industry signal.** A senior figure leaving a Big-6 manufacturer for a software-rehab startup is a directional indicator that the post-fitting gap is now seen as the underserved value pool.
2. **Aural rehab is being repositioned as standard of care.** Combined with Neurotone's prior 1,000-clinic milestone (April 2026), AI-driven aural rehab is no longer experimental.
3. **Software-first hearing care.** This is the inverse of the device-first model the industry has run on for 50 years — outcomes optimized through cognition/training rather than additional DSP horsepower.
4. **Recurring revenue model implication.** A clinic offering AI rehab as a subscription alongside hearing aids creates the kind of post-sale annuity stream that the industry has historically lacked.

## Relevance to Hearing + AI

- Highlights a category of "AI in hearing" that is **not** about device-level DNN sound processing but about training the **listener** (the brain side of the hearing-care problem).
- Complementary to on-device SE: better processing × better-trained listener could compound into outcomes that neither alone achieves.
- Builds the evidence-base argument for outcome metrics that go beyond audiometric thresholds — aligns with the same direction the Apple Hearing Study results push (subjective experience, real-world function as primary outcomes).
