# Sensorion Names SENS-601 (GJB2) as Lead Gene Therapy; Drops SENS-501 (OTOF)

## Bibliographic
- **Issuer:** Sensorion (Montpellier, France)
- **Date:** 9 Jun 2026
- **Coverage picked up by:** BusinessWire, Hearing Health & Technology Matters, BioSpace, BioPharma Dive, Precision Medicine Online, The Hearing Review
- **URL:** https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260609361868/en/

## Headline
Sensorion selects SENS-601, its AAV gene therapy for GJB2-related congenital hearing loss, as the company's lead program. Discontinues SENS-501 (OTOF) citing competitive crowding by Regeneron's Otarmeni and the comparatively smaller OTOF patient pool.

## Regulatory Status of SENS-601
- Clinical Trial Applications (CTAs) filed in Canada and France.
- Additional CTA filings planned in the United States and Australia by end of 2026.
- First-in-human dosing targeted off the back of those approvals.

## Why GJB2 Matters
- GJB2 encodes connexin 26, a gap-junction protein required for the K+ recycling and ionic homeostasis that underlie sound transduction in the cochlea.
- GJB2 mutations account for approximately 50% of autosomal-recessive non-syndromic hearing loss — the single largest genetic cause of congenital deafness.
- No approved treatments exist today — unmet need is an order of magnitude larger than OTOF.

## Why OTOF Was Dropped
- OTOF-related hearing loss is rare (estimates ~2-8% of prelingual severe-to-profound cases).
- Regeneron's Otarmeni gained FDA approval in 23 Apr 2026 (61-day National Priority Voucher pathway) — first approved gene therapy for any form of hearing loss.
- Sensorion explicitly cites a "notably changed development environment" — a polite framing of "the market window has closed".

## Technical Difference from OTOF Therapies
- OTOF therapy targets inner hair cells (structurally intact, only synaptic vesicle fusion missing).
- GJB2 therapy must restore gap-junction networks across supporting cells (Deiters' cells, inner border cells) and inner sulcus epithelium — a topologically harder restoration problem than restoring a single protein in a single cell type.
- Single AAV packaging fits GJB2 (cDNA ~681 bp), so a split-vector approach (required for OTOF's ~6 kb cDNA) is not needed.
