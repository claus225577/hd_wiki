# Sonova FY 2025/26 Full-Year Results

## Headline Figures

- **Group sales:** CHF 3,605.9 million, **+5.9% in local currencies** (Swiss-franc strength caused a −0.2% reported sales decline)
- **Normalized EBITA:** CHF 811.2 million, **+17.3% in local currencies** (+3.7% reported)
- **Net profit:** CHF 546 million
- **Basic EPS:** CHF 9.02
- **Proposed dividend:** **CHF 4.70 per share** (highest in company history; +7% YoY; ~45% payout ratio)
- **R&D spending:** **CHF 217.7 million, +3.8% in local currencies**
- **R&D-to-sales ratio:** ~6.0% (modestly compressed YoY)

## Segment Performance

- **Hearing Instruments (Wholesale + Retail):** Combined growth **+7.5% in local currencies** — market share gains continue
- **Cochlear Implants:** **−11.1% in local currencies**, particularly impacted by **China volume-based procurement (VBP)** pricing policies

## Strategic Developments

- **Consumer Hearing (Sennheiser brand) divestment:** Plans confirmed; reclassified as discontinued operations
- **Regionalized operating model:** Transition underway to enhance operational efficiency and market responsiveness
- **Product launches in fiscal year:** Infinio Ultra (October) with AutoSense OS 7.0, Sphere variants, Virto R Infinio (first rechargeable custom ITE), EasyGuard wax management system
- **CI / precision-pharma R&D:** Spiral Therapeutics equity + R&D collaboration (Advanced Bionics, April 2026)

## Guidance for FY 2026/27

- Consolidated sales: **+5–8%** at constant currencies
- Core EBIT: **+7–10%** at constant currencies

## Data-Science Reading

- AI feature surface clearly expanded in 2025/26 (Infinio Ultra, AutoSense 7.0, Sphere variants, Virto R Infinio, EasyGuard) against **single-digit R&D budget growth (+3.8% LC)** and a modestly compressed R&D-to-sales ratio
- Two non-exclusive interpretations:
  1. **AI R&D productivity dividend** — ML tooling and agentic-engineering practices raising work-per-engineer-hour in hearing-aid R&D (operational reading of the Karpathy "outsource execution, not understanding" framing)
  2. **Capitalization timing** — current year harvests prior platform investments; next platform cycle's bill arrives in 2026/27
- The two hypotheses are testable against next year's R&D-line trajectory — flat R&D + continuing output expansion supports (1); jump in R&D supports (2)
- The instrumentation question for any regulated medical-device OEM: what an internal AI R&D productivity dashboard looks like that is defensible to both a CFO and a notified body / FDA reviewer

## CI Segment Reading

- The hearing-aid (wholesale/retail) segment is a market-priced product where AI investments can be monetized via differentiated pricing
- The CI segment increasingly faces **government-procurement** pricing pressure (China VBP, similar tenders elsewhere)
- Implication for AI R&D capital allocation: differentiated returns to AI investment across segments — hearing-aid AI converts to margin, CI AI converts to access/volume
