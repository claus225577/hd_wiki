# Cilcare CIL001 US Tinnitus Trial Preparation at Mass General

## Summary

French biotech Cilcare announced (at the SelectUSA Investment Summit 2026, early May) preparation of a US clinical trial of its lead candidate **CIL001** at Massachusetts General Hospital in Boston. The trial will be run with **Prof. Stéphane Maison's** auditory neuroscience group and would be the **first US evaluation of CIL001 in chronic tinnitus patients**. Cilcare also announced a partnership with **CBSET** (preclinical / translational research org) to expand its US research capacity.

## Drug Mechanism

- **CIL001 targets cochlear synaptopathy** — degeneration of synapses between inner hair cells and auditory nerve fibers
- Cochlear synaptopathy is implicated in **tinnitus**, **hyperacusis**, and **"hidden hearing loss"** (poor speech-in-noise performance despite a normal audiogram)
- No FDA-approved pharmacotherapy exists for chronic tinnitus today — current management is behavioural (iCBT) or device-based (sound therapy, hearing aids, bimodal neuromodulation)

## Why This Matters

- A successful CIL001 readout would be the first **disease-modifying** tinnitus therapy — distinct from the symptomatic / coping interventions that dominate the current market
- Cochlear synaptopathy is also the leading candidate mechanism for "hidden hearing loss," which has no current diagnostic test and no treatment — a CIL001 success would validate the synaptopathy target across both indications
- Mass General + Maison collaboration adds credibility: Maison's lab at Harvard / MEEI has produced influential cochlear-synaptopathy work
- Sets up a pharma lane that runs **parallel to** (not in competition with) digital therapeutics like SilentCloud — different patient subsets, different evidence requirements

## Status

- **Trial preparation stage as of May 2026** — site selection and protocol work underway
- US regulatory pathway not yet disclosed in announcement
- Adds to Cilcare's existing DIAMANT trial program (biomarkers for hidden hearing loss in metabolic disorders, presented at ARO 2026)
