## Key Facts

- EHIMA member companies sold 23.16 million hearing aids globally in 2025, a +2.1% YoY increase vs 22.69M in 2024.
- Multi-year unit growth trajectory (EHIMA-reported):
  - 2021: 19.34M (+37.0% — post-pandemic rebound)
  - 2022: 20.25M (+4.7%)
  - 2023: 21.81M (+7.7%)
  - 2024: 22.69M (+4.0%)
  - 2025: 23.16M (+2.1%)
- Unit growth has *decelerated* for two consecutive years (7.7% → 4.0% → 2.1%) despite an unprecedented AI feature surge across all five major OEMs during 2024–2026.
- EHIMA president Peter Karlströmer quoted: figures "reflect increasing awareness of the importance of hearing health and the positive impact that professionally fitted hearing solutions can have on quality of life, social participation, and healthy ageing."
- Report covers EHIMA members only (the five major manufacturers — Sonova, Demant, WS Audiology, GN, Starkey — plus several smaller members). Excludes OTC-only entrants and most consumer audio competitors.
- No regional breakdown published in the headline release.
- No commentary on AI features, OTC channel impact, or market share dynamics.

## Why It Matters

This is the cleanest single industry-aggregate data point on whether AI feature investment is moving the adoption needle at the global unit level. The deceleration (from +7.7% to +2.1% in two years) lands in the same week as several premium AI launches (Widex Allure AI, Signia Active Mini IX) and one quarter after the AI feature density peak (Phonak Sphere Infinio, Oticon Zeal, ReSound Vivia, Starkey Omega all GA in the trailing year).

The natural reading: chip-side AI uplift is necessary but not sufficient for unit growth. The constraint sits upstream — awareness, distribution, workforce, stigma, friction at the door — none of which a 6 dB SNR uplift addresses.

Pair with EuroTrak UK 2025 (adoption rate fell from 52.8% to 50.5% despite higher satisfaction) for a coherent decoupling story.
