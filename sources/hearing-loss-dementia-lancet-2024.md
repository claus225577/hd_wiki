# Hearing Loss as the Largest Modifiable Dementia Risk Factor — Lancet 2024

## Key Extractions
- 2024 Lancet Commission identifies hearing loss as the single largest modifiable risk factor for dementia in mid-life
- ACHIEVE study (Johns Hopkins): hearing intervention slowed cognitive decline by 48% over 3 years
- Danish population study of 573,000 people: untreated hearing loss increases dementia risk by 7%
- Meta-analysis result: 37% increased dementia risk associated with hearing loss
- Hearing aid use may reduce the rate of cognitive decline by approximately 19%
- Mid-life intervention window identified as critical — earlier treatment linked to better cognitive outcomes
- Mechanisms proposed: cognitive load from effortful listening, social isolation, auditory cortex degradation
- Findings strengthen public health case for hearing screening and accessible hearing care

## Relevance
The 48% cognitive decline reduction from the ACHIEVE study is the strongest causal evidence yet linking hearing treatment to dementia prevention. This reframes hearing care as a neurological public health priority, not just a quality-of-life issue.
