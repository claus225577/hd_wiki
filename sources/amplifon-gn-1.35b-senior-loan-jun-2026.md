# Amplifon Secures €1.35B Senior Loan to Finance GN Hearing Acquisition

## Headline
Amplifon has secured a €1.35B senior loan to finance the cash component of its previously announced $2.64B (€2.3B) acquisition of GN Hearing. The financing milestone moves the transaction closer to its targeted end-2026 close.

## Deal Recap
- Total enterprise value: $2.64B (€2.3B).
- GN Store Nord receives €1.69B cash + 56M Amplifon shares at closing.
- GN becomes Amplifon's second-largest shareholder (~16% pro-forma).
- Combined revenue ~€3.3B; 20,000+ employees; 100+ countries.
- Targeted net EBITDA synergies €60-80M by 2029, mostly from insourcing Amplifon's own device volumes from GN's ReSound / Beltone / Jabra Enhance manufacturing.
- Closing targeted end-2026, subject to regulatory clearance.

## Why the Loan Is Worth Tracking Separately
- Senior debt financing of this size is the practical gate between "announced deal" and "closed deal" — securing it removes one of the largest residual execution risks.
- Establishes leverage profile of the combined entity going into the close and the 2027-29 integration window.
- Signals lender confidence in the cash-flow logic of a vertically integrated retail-plus-manufacturer hearing-care player — a structural bet on the OTC era not collapsing retail margins.

## Strategic Implications
- Amplifon transitions from world's largest hearing-aid retailer to vertically integrated manufacturer-retailer.
- Direct competitive pressure on Sonova, Demant/Oticon, WS Audiology/Signia — they now face a major customer (Amplifon stores) that owns a competing manufacturing arm.
- Data flywheel: Amplifon's retail patient data combined with GN's R&D pipeline is a meaningfully different position from any other competitor.
