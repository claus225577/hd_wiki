# Sony OTC Hearing Aids Discontinued — HearingTracker Coverage

## Key Extraction

- **Event:** Sony and WS Audiology ended OTC partnership after **3.5 years**
- **Products pulled:** CRE-E10, CRE-C10, CRE-C20 — all pulled from U.S. market
- **Performance paradox:** Despite **#1 HearAdvisor ranking**, commercial viability failed
- **Price range:** $700-$1,000 — premium OTC positioning
- **Key competitor:** Apple AirPods Pro 2/3 at $249/$200 with FDA hearing aid authorization
- **WSA's response:** Plans to remain in OTC market under different branding (not Sony)
- **Duration of partnership:** 3.5 years (launched ~late 2022)

## Significance

- **Product quality was not the problem:** #1 HearAdvisor ranking proves the devices were technically excellent
- **Price was the problem:** $700-$1,000 could not compete with AirPods Pro at $249 (now $200 for Pro 3)
- **Brand licensing model failed:** Sony brand trust was insufficient to justify 3-4x premium over Apple
- **Consumer behavior:** OTC buyers are price-sensitive; quality differentials don't justify premium pricing when Apple offers FDA-cleared alternative
- **WSA strategic pivot:** Signals intent to stay in OTC but with fundamentally different approach (likely lower price point, different brand)
