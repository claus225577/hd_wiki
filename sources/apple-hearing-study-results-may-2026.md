# Apple Hearing Study — May 2026 Results

## Key Extractions

### Study Overview
- **Run by:** Apple in collaboration with the University of Michigan School of Public Health
- **Participants:** 160,000+ consented adults across the United States
- **Status:** One of the largest hearing research studies ever conducted
- **Findings released:** 1 May 2026

### The Perception–Measurement Gap
- Sub-cohort of ~85,000 participants tested within WHO-defined normal hearing range (≤25 dB pure-tone average)
- **16% of these "clinically normal" participants rated their hearing as fair or poor**
- ≈13,500 individuals whose audiogram says "fine" but whose lived experience says otherwise
- Participants frequently reported difficulty concentrating in conversations and understanding speech in noise despite normal audiometric thresholds
- Researchers conclude the 4-frequency pure-tone average (4PTA), available via the AirPods Hearing Test, lets users monitor hearing drift even when classified clinically normal

### Mobility Sub-Analysis
- **N = 57,183** for the mobility analysis
- **Worse hearing was correlated with reduced walking speed**, with the strongest effect in adults aged 60+
- Walking speed is a well-validated predictor of healthspan: falls, hospitalization, cognitive decline, and all-cause mortality
- Apple frames the AirPods Hearing Aid feature as a candidate intervention to support physical health over the life course

### Direct Quote
"Enabling individuals to monitor their hearing using the 4PTA…gives them the opportunity to track their hearing as it changes over time, even when classified as clinically normal." (Apple Hearing Study findings, May 2026)

## Relevance

This is the largest population-scale dataset connecting subjective hearing experience and physical-function outcomes (gait speed) to objective audiometric measures. It pushes the field on three fronts:

1. **Outcome measures** — Suggests audiometric thresholds alone are insufficient as targets for hearing aid personalization and validation; subjective experience and real-world function carry independent signal.
2. **Longitudinal phenotyping** — Demonstrates feasibility of continuous, ecological hearing monitoring at population scale via consumer hardware (AirPods + iPhone) outside the clinic.
3. **Healthspan framing** — Adds population-level evidence to the case (already supported by ACHIEVE, Lancet Commission) that hearing intervention is a longevity intervention, not just an audibility intervention.

The findings also reinforce Apple's strategic position: a consumer-electronics company is now generating the kind of population-scale longitudinal hearing data that traditional hearing aid manufacturers and clinical research networks have been unable to assemble.
