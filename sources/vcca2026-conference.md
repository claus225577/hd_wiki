# VCCA2026 — 7th Virtual Conference on Computational Audiology

**Dates:** June 25–26, 2026 (pre-conference workshops June 19)
**Host location:** Spain (fully virtual)
**Format:** Free, fully virtual, all talks available offline on YouTube
**Submission deadline:** June 7, 2026

## Programme Structure

### Pre-Conference Workshops (June 18–19) — detail added 2026-06-20
- **Workshop 1 (Thu Jun 18):** Dr. Natàlia Gorina-Careta (UVic) + Dr. Helia Relaño-Iborra (Eriksholm / Rochester) — "From Question to Evidence: Building Your Research and Scientific Communication in Audiology"
- **Workshop 2 (Fri Jun 19):** Dr. Mark Saddler (DTU Hearing Systems, ex-McDermott lab MIT) — "A Practical Introduction to Machine Learning for Hearing Science" — hands-on Jupyter notebooks, materials on GitHub
- **Workshop 3 (Fri Jun 19):** Annesya Banerjee (Harvard / MIT) — "Computational Models of the Auditory Periphery: From Normal Hearing to Impairment" — interactive simulations, no coding required
- See standalone source: [[vcca2026-pre-conference-workshops-jun-19-2026]]

### Day 1 — June 25

**Room A:**
- Session 1.A: "Computational Audiology & Digital Hearing Research" — model-based approaches and data-driven insights (Chair: Dr. Helia Relaño Iborra)
- Session 2.A: "Speech-in-Noise and Auditory Scene Analysis" — perceptual mechanisms for real-world speech understanding (Chair: Dr. Miriam I. Marrufo-Pérez)
- Session 3.A: "Hidden Hearing Loss and Subclinical Auditory Disorders" — synaptopathy mechanisms and diagnostic markers (Chair: Dr. Joaquin T. Valderrama)

**Room B:**
- Session 1.B: "Binaural Hearing & Spatial Sound Perception" — localization and complex environments (Chair: Dr. Jaime Andres Undurraga Lucero)
- Session 2.B: "Cognition, Listening Effort & Brain Connectivity" — neuro-cognitive models and EEG/fMRI correlates (Chair: Dr. Yue Zhang)
- Session 3.B: "Objective Measures and Biomarkers" — electrophysiological and behavioral indices (Chair: Dr. Andrew J. Beynon)

### Day 2 — June 26

**Room A:**
- Session 4.A: "Machine Learning & AI for Hearing Science" (Chair: Prof. Dr. Tobias Goehring)
- Session 5.A: "Cochlear Implants: Challenges and Prospects" (Chair: Prof. Tania Hanekom)
- Session 6.A: "From Lab to Clinic: Ensuring Impact" (Chair: Dr. Ángel Ramos de Miguel)

**Room B:**
- Session 4.B: "Tele-Audiology and Next-Generation Clinical Tools" (Chair: Dr. Nikki Philpott)
- Session 5.B: "Hearing-Aid Innovation and Performance Assessment" (Chair: Dr. Raul Sanchez Lopez)
- Session 6.B: "Multisensory and Vestibular Integration" (Chair: Dr. Seba Ausili)

## Additional Features
- 4 keynote lectures across both days
- 4 invited speaker slots
- Poster blitz presentations for each session track
- Young Scientist Award

## Key Extraction

VCCA2026 introduces a dedicated session on "Hidden Hearing Loss and Subclinical Auditory Disorders" (synaptopathy), reflecting growing research interest in cochlear synaptopathy. The "From Lab to Clinic" session signals maturation of computational audiology from pure research toward clinical impact. Pre-conference ML workshops indicate growing demand for computational skills among audiologists.
