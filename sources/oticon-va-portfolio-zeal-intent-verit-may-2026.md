# Oticon Expands U.S. VA Portfolio (May 2026)

## Key Facts

- **Manufacturer:** Oticon (Demant)
- **Channel:** U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs supply schedule
- **Newly added devices:**
  - **Oticon Zeal** — rechargeable in-the-ear (ITE) hearing aid; AAA 2026 launch device
  - **Oticon Intent miniBTE R** — new BTE form factor on the Intent platform with Bluetooth LE Audio + Google Fast Pair
  - **Oticon Verit** — Demant's value-tier device added to the VA schedule
- **Underlying tech:** Sensor-driven BrainHearing technology + 2nd-gen AI sound processing (Intent platform); encapsulation-tech ITE shell (Zeal)

## Why This Is Notable

The VA is the largest single hearing-aid purchaser globally, supplying free hearing aids to ~1M veterans with hearing loss. Inclusion on the VA supply schedule is a structural revenue lever — it doesn't show up in retail launch headlines but it materially shifts unit volume.

Oticon's addition of Zeal (ITE), Intent miniBTE R (rechargeable BTE), and Verit (value-tier) gives VA audiologists a wider Oticon form-factor range than at any prior point. It reinforces the pattern of the major manufacturers competing on portfolio breadth in the institutional / public-health channels, in parallel with the OTC retail front.
