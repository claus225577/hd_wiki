# Samsung Galaxy S26 Audio Eraser: Real-Time Sound Separation

## Key Facts

- Samsung's **Galaxy S26** features **Audio Eraser** transitioning from post-production to **real-time sound separation**
- Previously, Audio Eraser was a post-capture editing feature — now operates during live audio capture
- Represents the move from offline processing to real-time on-device source separation
- Samsung promoting the feature but not confirming backward availability for S25/S24 — suggests it may require the S26's newer chipset (likely Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 2 or Exynos equivalent)

## Significance

- Real-time sound separation on a smartphone is technically adjacent to what hearing aids do for noise reduction
- Demonstrates that consumer smartphone chipsets now have sufficient ML inference capability for real-time audio AI
- The "post-production to real-time" transition mirrors the hearing aid industry's own evolution from offline fitting to real-time DNN processing
- Consumer exposure to AI audio features (Audio Eraser, Apple Clean Up, Google Audio Magic Eraser) normalizes expectations for hearing device AI
- If smartphones can do real-time source separation, the question becomes: why can't hearing aids do more?

## Relevance to Hearing + AI

- Consumer benchmark: sets user expectations for what AI audio should do in real-time
- Technology transfer potential: smartphone-grade source separation models could be distilled for hearing aid deployment
- Competitive pressure: as phones gain hearing-aid-like capabilities, prescription devices must demonstrate superior performance to justify price premium
- Part of the convergence trend where consumer electronics and hearing devices share overlapping AI audio capabilities
