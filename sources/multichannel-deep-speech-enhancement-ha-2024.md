# Real-Time Multichannel Deep Speech Enhancement in Hearing Aids

## Summary

Compares monaural and binaural deep speech enhancement processing in complex acoustic scenarios for hearing aids. Published in IEEE/ACM Transactions on Audio, Speech and Language Processing.

## Key Contributions

- Direct comparison of monaural vs. binaural DNN processing pipelines
- Evaluated in complex, realistic acoustic scenarios (not just simple lab conditions)
- Real-time capable architecture
- Demonstrates that binaural processing preserves spatial cues while enhancing speech — critical for hearing aid users who rely on spatial hearing

## Significance

Binaural processing in hearing aids is essential for preserving interaural time and level differences (ITD/ILD) that enable sound localization. This paper shows that deep learning enhancement can maintain these cues while still improving SNR — solving a key limitation of earlier DNN approaches that processed each ear independently.
