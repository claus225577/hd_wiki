# Veterans Hearing Aid Improvement Act — House Companion Introduced (June 1, 2026)

On **June 1, 2026**, Reps. **Kevin Mullin (D-CA-15)** and **Keith Self (R-TX-03)** introduced the **Veterans Hearing Aid Improvement Act** in the U.S. House of Representatives. The bill is the House companion to **S. 3739**, previously introduced in the Senate by **Sen. Marsha Blackburn (R-TN)** and **Sen. Adam Schiff (D-CA)**.

## What the Bill Does

- Establishes a **two-year pilot program** within the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) to evaluate the use of **FDA-cleared over-the-counter (OTC) hearing aids** for veterans with **mild-to-moderate hearing loss**.
- Maintains **clinical supervision and VA oversight** of OTC dispensing.
- Targets veterans who currently face wait times or geographic barriers to traditional VA audiology fitting.

## Why It Matters

- **Hearing loss is the #1 service-connected disability** among American veterans (per VA OIG and VHA reports cited in the press release).
- The VA is the **largest U.S. single-purchaser of hearing aids** (~18% of U.S. units in 2022; ~700K aids/year recent baseline). Today, OEM access to that channel is via the VA Federal Supply Schedule (FSS) prescription-tier procurement, dispensed by VA audiologists.
- An OTC pilot opens a **second procurement-and-dispensing pathway** inside the VA that the existing OEM cluster (Oticon Zeal/Intent/Verit, Signia Active Mini IX, Starkey Omega AI Government Services) was not designed for.
- Aligns with the broader 2026 thesis that hearing-care access is workforce-bound: the AAA 2026 leadership panel and VA audiology staffing data both flag audiologist supply as the binding constraint.

## Implications

### For OEMs
- Premium-AI VA cluster (Oticon / Signia / Starkey) was sized for the **prescription tier**. The pilot opens an adjacent tier in the same channel without displacing the existing contracts.
- **OTC-channel OEMs** without VA presence (Apple AirPods Pro 2/3, Lexie, Eargo, Ceretone, Audien) gain a structurally new addressable population if FDA-cleared OTC devices become VA-pilot-eligible.
- The pilot is structured as evaluative — outcome data from VA audiology supervision is a **standardized-outcomes dataset** on OTC use in a clinically-supervised setting, the first of its kind at this scale.

### For the VA-as-Dataset Synthesis
- Adds a **third operational tier** (after prescription premium and prescription value) to the VA hearing-aid channel.
- If the pilot is structured with CPRS-side outcome capture (APHAB, COSI, HHIE-S), it produces the first systematic comparison of **supervised OTC** vs. **prescription** fitting in a unified EHR — directly relevant to the open question on the VA synthesis page about how OEM channel structure shapes outcome-bridge feasibility.

### For OTC Policy
- The Sony OTC withdrawal (April 2026) and the live Lexie B3 / Eargo 8 mid-/upper-tier experiments leave open the question of whether **clinically-supervised OTC** is a more sustainable category than purely-retail OTC. A VA pilot is the natural test bed.

## Open Questions

1. Will the bill be referred to the House VA Committee or Energy & Commerce? Jurisdiction affects timeline.
2. Will the pilot's RFP specify **outcome-data telemetry return** as a vendor requirement, or stay procurement-neutral?
3. Will Apple seek pilot inclusion for AirPods Pro 2/3? The FDA-clearance + iOS Hearing Aid Feature combination is now eligible under any reasonable reading of "FDA-cleared OTC hearing aid."
4. Does this open a regulatory precedent for **clinically-supervised OTC** as a distinct dispensing model — i.e., a third lane between pure-retail OTC and prescription?
