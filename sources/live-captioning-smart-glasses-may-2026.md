# Live-Captioning Smart Glasses — May 2026 Roundup

## Key Extraction

- **What:** A May 2026 hands-on comparison (originating from Wired's review series) of the current crop of live-captioning AR/smart glasses, naming a single clear leader on caption accuracy and end-to-end latency.
- **Form factor:** Glasses with on-board (or phone-tethered) microphone arrays + ASR + display optics rendering live captions in the wearer's field of view.
- **Use case:** Real-time speech-to-text for the wearer in conversational and lecture settings — increasingly framed as an alternative or complement to hearing aids for mild–moderate users and as an adjunct for severe loss / single-sided deafness / tinnitus where amplification alone is insufficient.

## Why This Matters for Hearing AI

- **Category convergence.** Live-captioning glasses are now closing the gap with hearing aids on the "understand speech in noise" job-to-be-done — but via display rather than amplification. This puts pressure on HA OEMs to either (a) ship their own caption-rendering peripherals or (b) interoperate cleanly with glasses.
- **Source of training data.** Glasses-class caption pipelines run heavyweight ASR models (often cloud-assisted) that produce gold-standard transcripts of the user's day; if HA telemetry can be co-registered with these transcripts, it enables a much richer outcome-measurement loop than any HA-only system.
- **Accessibility regulatory tailwind.** Section 508 / EAA / WCAG 2.2 trends increasingly recognize captions as a primary accommodation. Glasses move that accommodation from venue-installed (Auracast assistive listening) to user-carried — different procurement path.
