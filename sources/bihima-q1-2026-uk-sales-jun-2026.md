# BIHIMA Q1 2026 UK Hearing Aid Sales

## Headline Numbers

- **UK private-sector sales: 145,575 units in Q1 2026**, vs **129,371 in Q4 2025** → **+12.5% QoQ**
- **Rechargeable share: 93%** (135,847 units) of private sales — fully dominant form factor
- Reported by the **British and Irish Hearing Instrument Manufacturers Association (BIHIMA)**

## Why This Matters

- Confirms **rechargeable as the de facto UK private-sector standard** — disposable-battery models now a residual ~7% niche
- 12.5% QoQ growth in private channel suggests demand resilience independent of NHS provisioning
- Forms a counterpoint to global EHIMA 2025 numbers and to Demant's Q1 2026 Zeal results — UK private-pay market is healthy and rechargeable-locked
- Rechargeable lock-in matters for **on-device AI**: battery-budget headroom for DNN inference is paired with the rechargeable form factor, since persistent zinc-air swaps would not sustain modern always-on neural processing
