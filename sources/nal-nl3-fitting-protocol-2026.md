# NAL-NL3 — Next-Generation Hearing-Aid Fitting Prescription

## Summary
The National Acoustic Laboratories (NAL) is rolling out **NAL-NL3**, the successor to the long-standing NAL-NL2 prescriptive fitting target. Announced through audiology trade press in April 2026.

## What Changes vs. NAL-NL2
- Refined gain prescriptions across the audiogram, particularly for **complex hearing-loss configurations** (e.g., reverse-slope, cookie-bite, severe high-frequency drop-offs).
- Introduces a **new philosophy for noisy environments** — explicitly trades off speech intelligibility against listening comfort, rather than maximizing intelligibility at the cost of fatigue.
- Backed by the (separately-published) Lansbergen et al. *Trends in Hearing* finding that **deviations from NAL-NL2 targets at 4–8 kHz** show a small but reliable association with self-reported benefit — i.e., the high-frequency region matters more than NL2 implicitly assumed.

## Why It Matters
- NAL targets are the *de facto* default for non-proprietary fittings worldwide. Any change ripples through every fitting software platform (Phonak Target, Oticon Genie, Signia Connexx, GN ReSound Smart Fit).
- Creates a friction window: software vendors need to ship NL3 calculators; audiologists need re-training; existing patients fitted with NL2 face a "do we re-fit?" decision.
- The "comfort vs. intelligibility" reframe aligns with the AI/DNN-driven trend toward **listener-state-aware processing** — i.e., fitting goals as a function of context, not a single static target.
