# Hearing Loss, Brain Structure, and Dementia Risk — Framingham

## Summary

Large-scale epidemiological study from the Framingham Heart Study examining associations between hearing loss, brain structural changes (MRI), cognitive performance, and dementia risk. Published in JAMA Network Open, 2024.

## Key Findings

- Hearing loss associated with changes in brain structure visible on MRI
- Longitudinal tracking of hearing loss → cognitive decline → dementia trajectory
- Builds on the Lancet Commission finding that hearing loss is the largest modifiable risk factor for dementia (7% of population attributable risk)

## Significance

Adds neuroimaging evidence to the epidemiological link between hearing loss and dementia. The brain structural changes associated with hearing loss may be early biomarkers that predict future cognitive decline — potential targets for AI-based risk stratification.
