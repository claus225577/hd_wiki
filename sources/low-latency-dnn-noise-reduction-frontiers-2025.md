## Key Facts

- Single-channel time-domain audio separation model achieves 1ms processing latency, enabling use in real-time hearing devices without perceptible delay.
- Benefits demonstrated across three listener populations: normal hearing, hearing-impaired, and cochlear implant (CI) users.
- Time-domain approach (operating on raw waveforms rather than spectrograms) avoids phase reconstruction artifacts and reduces algorithmic delay.
- CI users showed particular benefit due to the already limited spectral resolution of electrode stimulation; cleaner input signal improves electrode mapping quality.
- Results suggest a single trained model can serve multiple hearing device form factors without population-specific retraining.
