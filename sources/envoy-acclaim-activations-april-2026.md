# Envoy Medical Acclaim — All 56 Pivotal-Trial Patients Activated

## Key Facts
- Envoy Medical announced on 22 April 2026 that all 56 patients in the pivotal trial of the **fully implanted Acclaim® Cochlear Implant** have completed their **Activation Visits** (initial device turn-on).
- The Acclaim has FDA **Breakthrough Device Designation** (granted 2019).
- Distinct from external-microphone CIs, Acclaim uses an **implanted sensor that leverages the natural anatomy of the ear** to capture sound — no external mic, no processor worn behind the ear.
- FDA earlier expanded the trial to add 46 more patients; full enrollment of the expansion targeted early 2026.

## Significance
- Removes the chief social/lifestyle pain points of conventional CIs: visible external hardware, water exposure, battery management on the head.
- Sensor-based input opens a different signal-chain design space — closer to natural ear biomechanics; pre-processing must compensate for impedance/dynamic-range characteristics of in-ear sensing rather than external acoustic capture.
- Pivotal-trial completion is the gating step before FDA marketing-authorization filing.

## Connections
- Competitor framing: Cochlear Ltd. Nucleus 8 / Nexa, Advanced Bionics, MED-EL — all still externally-worn processor architectures.
- Adjacent trend: completely-implantable cochlear implants is a multi-vendor research arc (see `completely-implantable-cochlear-implants-april-2026.md`).
