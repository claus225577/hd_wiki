# Best Over-the-Counter Hearing Aids — Consumer Reports (Jun 2026)

## Bibliographic
- **Publication:** Consumer Reports
- **Article URL:** https://www.consumerreports.org/health/hearing-aids/best-over-the-counter-hearing-aids-review-a7906529120/
- **Latest update:** 12 Jun 2026

## Scope
First Consumer Reports head-to-head of 14 OTC hearing aids, covering:
- Apple (AirPods Pro / Pro 3 with OTC Hearing Aid Feature)
- Eargo
- Lexie
- Sennheiser
- MDHearing, Audien Hearing, Yeasound, Soundbright, Elehear, Concha Labs

## Methodology
- Professional audiology lab measurement (noise-filtering, sound-quality spec compliance) combined with CR's user-experience panel.
- Panellists with hearing loss participated in listening tests and comfort evaluations.
- Three evaluation axes: ease of use, performance, sound quality.
- Smartphone-app usability scored where applicable.

## Headline Finding
OTC has materially lowered access for mild-to-moderate hearing loss, but quality and price-vs-performance vary widely across the catalogue. CR explicitly recommends using the manufacturer trial period and contacting customer support during testing rather than treating any single OTC aid as turnkey.

## Why It Matters
- Apple is now folded into a mainstream consumer-products test as a default comparator alongside historical OTC pure-plays (Eargo, Lexie, Sennheiser). Signals the de facto consumer-electronics-isation of the OTC tier.
- First CR-scale comparative test sets a benchmark methodology that hearing-aid OEMs and ENTs will be asked about by consumers in 2026–2027.
- Reinforces the OTC trial-period UX as the binding bottleneck — not regulatory access — for OTC adoption.

## Open Questions / Limitations
- CR does not publish individual scores per axis in the public abstract; full ratings paywalled.
- No direct prescription-vs-OTC comparator in the same test (a recurring CR limitation).
