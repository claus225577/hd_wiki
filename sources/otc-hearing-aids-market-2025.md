## Key Facts

- FDA OTC hearing aid rule became effective October 2022, opening the US market to direct-to-consumer devices for mild-to-moderate hearing loss.
- As of February 2025, 142 OTC hearing aid products available on the US market.
- Three device classes: self-fitting (programmed via smartphone app by user), preset-based (fixed programs), and software-based (PC/app configuration).
- Output limits: maximum 111 dB SPL, or 117 dB SPL with compression enabled — regulatory guardrail preventing inadvertent overamplification.
- Price range: $199–$999 per pair, dramatically below traditional prescription devices.
- Eligible population: adults 18+ with perceived mild-to-moderate hearing loss; not indicated for severe/profound loss or pediatric use.
- Key players entering OTC space include Sony, Jabra, Lexie, and pharmacy-brand white-label products.
- Market expected to grow as AI-powered self-fitting apps improve and consumer awareness increases.
