## Event details
- Location: Kind Seoul (jazz club) — **first venue in South Korea with permanent Auracast installation**
- Equipment: Auri Transmitter broadcasting to Auri receivers
- Performance: Big Ocean — K-pop trio whose members all live with hearing loss (Jiseok deaf since birth, hearing aids; Chanyeon began losing hearing at 11, bilateral cochlear implants; Park Hyunjin lost hearing at age 4, uses both an implant and a hearing aid)
- Panel: "Accessibility in entertainment" panel discussion followed the performance
- Confirmed permanent: Andreas Anderhov (GN APAC President) stated the installation is "not a one-time event"
- Context: World Congress of Audiology 2026 Seoul, May 24-27

## YouGov stigma data (key extraction)
- Methodology: Nationally representative online surveys conducted 30 January – 4 February 2026
- Sample: Australia (n=1,014, including n=312 with hearing loss); UK (n=1,023, n=324); US (n=1,016, n=319); total n≈3,053
- Headline numbers:
  - **Over one-third** of people with hearing loss report being perceived as "rude or disengaged"
  - **Up to 40%** report being viewed as "less intelligent or less capable"
  - **More than one-third** report others believe they should "try harder" to hear

## Why this matters
- **Stigma as a missing variable in hearing-aid adoption funnel models**: time-to-first-fitting (~7-10 years), candidacy uptake (~20% of eligible adults), and year-one abandonment are all stigma-driven outcomes. The hearing-aid R&D stack has been built on the supply side (chips, DNNs, fitting) for a decade. Stigma has never been instrumented as a model input.
- **First Asian Auracast venue install**: Geographic and cultural deployment outside the EU/US axis. Korea has the demographic profile (population aging fast, audiologist shortage relative to demand) where infrastructure-side accessibility is the bottleneck.
- **Visibility-positive cultural intervention**: Big Ocean performing while wearing hearing aids/cochlear implants is structurally an intervention on the stigma variable, not a product launch. Industry has very few instruments for this kind of intervention; PSA campaigns (Listen Up Kentucky, May 2026) are the only existing comparable.
- **Trinity of recent stigma-adjacent moves**: Listen Up Kentucky PSA campaign (May 2026), ASHA NSLHM 2026 care-partner focus (May 2026), GN Seoul Session (May 26, 2026) — three independent industry signals that the supply-side optimization frontier may have hit diminishing returns and demand-side / sociological levers are now where the marginal gain lives.

## Open questions
- Will a stigma-aware adoption model become a publishable research direction? FUEL framework (Pichora-Fuller) is the closest existing conceptual scaffold but hasn't been operationalized as a hearing-aid telemetry signal.
- Three inputs not currently logged that would build such a model: cultural visibility events (performances, media coverage), partner/family attitudes (proxy via care-partner enrollment data), post-fitting social environment (acoustic context + conversation count via on-device telemetry).
- Does the permanent-install status at Kind Seoul become a measurement substrate (foot traffic, dwell time, repeat visits) that can be A/B compared with non-Auracast comparable venues?
