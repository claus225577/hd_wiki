## Key Facts

- 87.5% of all AI-in-audiology manuscripts were published in the last 4 years (as of review date), reflecting rapid acceleration of the field.
- Logistic regression applied to automated audiometry interpretation, reducing need for specialist involvement in screening contexts.
- CNNs used for radiological image analysis — e.g., detecting vestibular schwannoma, otosclerosis, and cholesteatoma on CT/MRI.
- LLMs applied to automatic diagnostic report generation from audiological data, with potential to reduce clinician documentation burden.
- Ethical challenges identified: lack of diversity in training datasets (most data from high-income, Western populations), risk of algorithmic bias in underserved communities.
- Call for standardization of outcome measures and data sharing frameworks to support larger, more generalizable training corpora.
- Future directions include multimodal AI (combining audiogram, imaging, and patient-reported data) and real-world evidence studies.
