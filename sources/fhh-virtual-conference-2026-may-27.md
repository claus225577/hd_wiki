# FHH Virtual Conference 2026 — Day 3 Wrap

## Event

Sixth annual **Future of Hearing Healthcare (FHH) Conference**, run virtually by HHTM over three half-day sessions: **May 13, May 20, and May 27, 2026**. Day 3 closed yesterday.

## Scope

- **23+ expert-led sessions** plus panel discussions.
- Track themes: clinical best practices, emerging technologies, research developments, evolving care models, AI in clinical workflows, OTC dynamics, third-party payer impacts.
- Provider-focused — recordings to roll out post-event.

## Editorial Framing

Day 3 closes the third major industry conversation in three weeks that put **AI front and centre**:

1. **AAA 2026 Leadership Panel** (April, San Antonio) — Sonova / Demant / GN / WSA / Starkey / Cochlear US presidents.
2. **WCA 2026 Seoul** (May 24–27) — Edwards / Launer / Strophal AI-in-clinic discussion.
3. **FHH 2026 Virtual** (May 13/20/27) — multi-session, provider-side.

Common pattern across all three: **AI is no longer treated as a forecast item** — it's already in the patient journey (engagement, fitting, outcome prediction, post-fitting counseling). The unresolved questions are about **clinician trust, patient-benefit evidence, and informed consent** rather than raw capability. This is the second consecutive month where the dominant tension in industry discourse is *interpretive* rather than *technical* — a maturity signal for the AI-in-hearing-care subfield.
