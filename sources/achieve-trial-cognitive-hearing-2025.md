# ACHIEVE Trial: Hearing Intervention and Cognitive Decline

## Summary

Secondary analysis of the Aging and Cognitive Health Evaluation in Elders (ACHIEVE) randomized controlled trial. Examines whether hearing intervention reduces cognitive decline, and whether benefits vary by baseline risk of cognitive decline. N=2,692 dementia-free participants from the ARIC cohort.

## Key Findings

- Hearing intervention did not reduce 3-year cognitive decline in ALL older adults with hearing loss
- BUT: High-risk subgroup (ARIC participants) showed hearing intervention halved the rate of cognitive decline over 3 years compared to control
- Benefits are stratified by baseline cognitive risk — personalized prediction of who benefits is important
- Age-related hearing loss accounts for ~7% of dementia risk (largest single modifiable risk factor per Lancet Commission)

## Significance

This is the highest-quality evidence to date (RCT) on whether treating hearing loss prevents cognitive decline. The stratified finding — that only high-risk individuals benefit significantly — has major implications for precision audiology: AI prediction models could identify who would benefit most from early hearing intervention for cognitive protection.
