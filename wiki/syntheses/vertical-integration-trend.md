# Vertical Integration Trend in the Hearing Industry

The hearing aid industry is consolidating around vertically integrated models where manufacturers also control retail distribution. The Amplifon-GN Hearing deal (€2.3B, 2026) is the latest and most dramatic example — and the first time a pure retailer has acquired a major manufacturer, inverting the typical direction of integration.

## Timeline of Vertical Integration

1. **Sonova acquires AudioNova (2016)** — Major manufacturer buys major retailer
2. **Demant builds HearingLife** — Organic growth of retail network in the US
3. **WS Audiology (Sivantos + Widex merge, 2019)** — Manufacturing consolidation, limited retail
4. **Amplifon acquires GN Hearing (2026, €2.3B)** — Retailer acquires manufacturer; first of its kind

## The Amplifon-GN Deal in Detail

The Amplifon-GN acquisition is structurally different from prior deals:

- **Direction:** A retailer (Amplifon, world's largest hearing care network) acquires a manufacturer (GN Hearing, maker of ReSound and Beltone brands)
- **Price:** €2.3B
- **What Amplifon gets:** Proprietary product manufacturing, vertically integrated supply chain, ReSound/Beltone IP and R&D
- **What the industry gets:** A new integrated competitor that controls product design through point-of-sale, rivaling Sonova and Demant

### GN Group's Strategic Pivot

Following the sale of GN Hearing, GN Group is pivoting its corporate focus to:
- **Audio/video peripherals:** Jabra headsets, conference room systems, video collaboration hardware
- **Enterprise communications:** B2B focus rather than medical device consumer market
- This represents a deliberate exit from the medically-regulated hearing aid market in favor of higher-margin enterprise AV

### Impact on Competitive Landscape

The deal reshapes the industry into three integrated "super-competitors":
1. **Sonova** — Phonak/Unitron manufacturing + AudioNova retail
2. **Demant** — Oticon/Bernafon manufacturing + HearingLife retail
3. **Amplifon + GN** — ReSound/Beltone manufacturing + Amplifon's global retail network

WS Audiology (Signia, Widex) and Starkey are now the outliers without significant owned retail.

## What's Driving Integration

1. **Data advantage** — Controlling both ends creates a closed-loop data flywheel; fitting outcomes, return rates, and satisfaction data stay proprietary
2. **Margin capture** — Retail margins (audiologist visits, fittings, accessories) exceed manufacturing margins
3. **Consumer experience** — End-to-end control enables seamless user journeys and brand loyalty
4. **OTC disruption** — Apple/Bose entry forces traditional players to differentiate on service, not just hardware
5. **Scale economies** — Combined R&D + distribution reduces cost per unit and accelerates product iteration

## Strategic Positions After Amplifon-GN

| Company | Manufacturing | Retail | Integrated? | Pressure Points |
|---------|--------------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Sonova | Phonak, Unitron | AudioNova | Yes | Sustaining DEEPSONIC lead |
| Demant | Oticon, Bernafon | HearingLife | Yes | 4D Sensor differentiation |
| Amplifon + GN | ReSound, Beltone | Amplifon network | Yes (new) | Integration execution risk |
| WS Audiology | Signia, Widex | Limited retail | Partially | No major retail anchor; building software-defined data flywheel via Sound Preference Program |
| Starkey | Starkey | Limited retail | Partially | Health ecosystem niche |

## Implications for Independent Audiologists

- **Channel squeeze:** As manufacturer-owned clinics grow, independent audiologists face brand loyalty programs that steer patients to integrated networks
- **Pricing pressure:** Vertically integrated players can subsidize device cost with service margin, undercutting independents on device price
- **Countermeasure:** Independent audiologist coalitions and buying groups (e.g., Audiology Distribution) exist specifically to maintain negotiating leverage
- **OTC as relief valve:** Apple/OTC growth creates a segment that bypasses both integrated networks and independents — could paradoxically help independents by preserving the complex-case market

## What Comes Next

- **WS Audiology or Starkey acquisition of retail chain** — The pressure on non-integrated players is acute; a WS Audiology retail acquisition would not be surprising in 2026–2027
- **Costco as wildcard** — Costco is the largest single hearing aid retailer in the US, selling ~15% of US hearing aids. A manufacturer partnership or exclusivity deal with Costco would reshape the non-integrated segment.
- **DTC brands** — Eargo, Jabra Enhance (if GN retains it post-acquisition), and Audicus operate direct-to-consumer without traditional retail — may gain share as integrated networks focus on premium segments
