# Acousia Therapeutics

**Acousia Therapeutics GmbH** is a Tübingen-based biopharmaceutical company developing small-molecule drugs for inner-ear indications. Founded out of the Tübingen ENT / hearing-research cluster. Its lead programme is **Bimokalner (ACOU085)**, a Kv7.4 (KCNQ4) potassium-channel modulator delivered as a transtympanic slow-release gel.

## Headquarters and Clinical Site

- **Headquarters:** Tübingen, Germany.
- **Phase 2a clinical site:** Tübingen University Medical Center.

Tübingen has become an OHC-biology cluster: Acousia (pharmacology side), the Marlies Knipper lab (hidden-HL diagnostic side, see [[../concepts/hidden-hearing-loss]]), and Tübingen University Medical Center (clinical-trial site) sit in the same city. Comparable in concentration to Oldenburg (HA signal processing) or Cambridge (CI signal processing).

## Lead Programme — Bimokalner (ACOU085)

- **Mechanism:** Kv7.4 (KCNQ4) potassium-channel modulator. Activates the channel whose loss-of-function mutations cause DFNA2 progressive HL. See [[../concepts/kv74-kcnq4-outer-hair-cell]].
- **Formulation:** transtympanic slow-release gel. Single application through the tympanic membrane; round-window-membrane diffusion into the cochlea. Local delivery, not systemic.
- **Phase 2a ProHear Study — readout 18 June 2026:**
  - Exploratory, placebo-controlled, **split-body** design — one ear treated with ACOU085, contralateral ear placebo, within the same patient.
  - **Population:** testicular cancer patients receiving cisplatin 300 mg/m² × 3 cycles — high-event-rate clean-signal ototoxicity setting.
  - **>90% of participants developed ototoxicity in at least one ear** — EHF 10–16 kHz, mild-to-moderate.
  - **Treated ear: clinically meaningful prevention of PTA threshold shifts vs placebo.**

## Next Steps

- Partnership discussions scheduled at **BIO International Convention, San Diego, 22–25 June 2026** — immediately following the Phase 2a readout.
- Phase 2b/3 expansion presumably contingent on partnership / financing outcomes.

## Strategic Position

### Most advanced clinical-stage OHC-side otoprotection programme

By mid-2026 Acousia is the clearest single example of pharmacological prevention of acquired sensorineural hearing loss reaching clinical Phase 2a positive readout. The combination of (a) the clean cisplatin signal, (b) the within-subject split-body design, and (c) the EHF-range outcome makes it one of the most interpretable inner-ear clinical readouts of the year.

### Upstream of the entire hearing-aid market

Bimokalner does not compete with hearing aids; it operates upstream of the candidate population. If pharmacological prevention scales beyond the chemo-induced setting (large-cohort age-related or noise-induced indications), the 2030–2040 candidate-population assumptions baked into HA market projections need re-grading. See [[../syntheses/hearing-aid-market-outlook]].

### Operational link to the diagnostic side

The Phase 2a success operationalises EHF audiometry (10–16 kHz) as a billable, FDA-relevant outcome — pulling the standard-of-care audiogram one full octave higher than the conventional 0.25–8 kHz range. The same EHF stack is what the [[../concepts/hidden-hearing-loss]] diagnostic programme has been calling out as the under-instrumented frequency range.

### Geographic / scientific coupling to Knipper lab

The Knipper lab at Tübingen has been the most active European voice on hidden hearing loss / cognitive risk (see [[../sources/knipper-hidden-hearing-loss-dementia-may-2026]]). Acousia is the pharmacological commercialisation of approximately the same OHC-biology framework. The Tübingen cluster is becoming the OHC-side analogue of what Mass Eye and Ear is for the OTOF gene therapy programme.

## Open Questions

- Phase 2b/3 design — does Acousia maintain the within-subject split-body or move to parallel-group? Statistical power tradeoffs against regulatory familiarity.
- Indication expansion strategy — chemo only, or attempt aminoglycoside / noise-induced / age-related concurrently?
- Partner profile from BIO 2026 — big pharma audiology-adjacent (rare); hearing-device OEM (Sonova / Demant / GN / Cochlear) is the more strategic but historically pharma-shy counterpart; specialty rare-disease pharma is the most plausible match.
- Manufacturing — transtympanic slow-release gel is a specialised formulation; CMO supply substrate is non-trivial at scale.
- Eventual interaction with the [[../concepts/gene-therapy-hearing]] thread — does Kv7.4 small-molecule activation get displaced by AAV-delivered KCNQ4 gene therapy on a 10-year horizon, or do the two coexist for different population segments (acquired-ototoxic vs DFNA2-genetic)?
