# Apple Hearing Features

Apple's expansion into hearing health through AirPods and Apple Watch, representing the biggest potential disruption to the traditional hearing aid industry.

## Current Features (as of 2026)
- **Hearing Test** — Clinical-grade audiogram via AirPods Pro 2 (FDA-cleared)
- **Hearing Aid Mode** — OTC hearing aid functionality in AirPods Pro 2 (FDA-cleared)
- **Noise exposure monitoring** — Via Apple Watch
- **Conversation Boost** — Amplification feature for face-to-face conversations
- **Live Listen** — Use iPhone as remote microphone

## Why This Matters
- AirPods installed base: hundreds of millions vs. ~15M hearing aid users globally
- FDA clearance as a Class II medical device legitimizes consumer electronics as hearing devices
- Price point (~$249) dramatically undercuts traditional hearing aids ($2,000-7,000/pair)
- Normalizes ear-worn technology — reduces stigma barrier

## Limitations vs. Traditional Hearing Aids
- Not for severe/profound hearing loss
- Battery life much shorter under hearing aid processing
- No professional fitting/verification workflow
- Limited fine-tuning compared to prescription devices

## Industry Impact
- Accelerates OTC hearing aid market
- Forces traditional manufacturers to compete on consumer experience
- May expand the addressable market by reaching mild-loss users who wouldn't visit an audiologist
- Creates competitive pressure on pricing and UX
