# HIMSA (Hearing Instrument Manufacturers' Software Association)

Industry consortium that develops and maintains the Noah standard — the de facto data exchange platform for audiometric data and hearing aid fitting across the global hearing care industry.

## Noah Platform

### Noah Desktop (Legacy)
- Traditional desktop software installed in audiology clinics
- Stores patient audiometric data, hearing aid fitting parameters, and session records
- Acts as the middleware between audiometric equipment and hearing aid programming software
- Used by virtually all major hearing aid manufacturers' fitting software (Genie 2, Target, Smart Fit, etc.)

### Noah ES (Cloud)
- Cloud-based evolution of Noah desktop
- **5,400+ subscribers globally** as of April 15, 2026 — **doubled year-over-year**
- Surpassed the **5,000-user milestone** in April 2026
- Enables cloud-based patient data management, remote access, and multi-location practice management
- Standardized cloud data format enables ML training on diverse patient populations

## Significance for AI in Hearing Care

Noah ES reaching 5,400+ cloud subscribers represents critical infrastructure for AI-driven audiology:

1. **Population-level analytics:** Cloud-aggregated audiometric data enables pattern discovery across demographics, hearing loss types, and fitting outcomes
2. **AI-assisted fitting:** Sufficient cloud data to train recommendation models for hearing aid parameters based on audiogram profiles and patient characteristics
3. **Federated learning potential:** Could support privacy-preserving ML across practices without sharing raw patient data
4. **Teleaudiology enablement:** Cloud infrastructure is prerequisite for remote fitting and follow-up care
5. **Longitudinal tracking:** Cloud storage enables tracking patient outcomes over years, not just single-visit snapshots

## Growth Trajectory

The doubling of Noah ES users year-over-year (to 5,400+ by April 2026) indicates accelerating cloud adoption in audiology. This growth rate suggests:
- Cloud-first hearing care practices are becoming the norm, not the exception
- The audiologist workforce shortage (75% of US counties are shortage areas) is pushing practices toward cloud-enabled remote care models
- Manufacturer support for Noah ES integration is broad enough to make the switch practical
