# Cochlear Ltd.

Australian medical device company; the largest cochlear implant manufacturer globally with approximately 50% of the U.S. CI market and 700,000+ devices implanted worldwide across 40+ years of operation. Headquartered in Sydney, Australia.

## Product Lines

### Nucleus Nexa System (Launched 2025)
- World's first cochlear implant with upgradeable firmware and on-implant internal memory
- See [[../sources/cochlear-nucleus-nexa-smart-implant-2026]] for full technical details
- Available to U.S. veterans through VA clinics as of January 8, 2026
- Featured prominently at AAA 2026 (San Antonio) — see [[aaa-conference]]
- Companion sound processor: Nucleus 8 Nexa — described as the world's smallest and lightest CI sound processor with all-day battery life

### Fully Implantable CI Program
- 21 years of R&D on a fully-implantable cochlear implant with subcutaneous microphone
- Status: in development, no public clinical timeline yet
- See [[../concepts/cochlear-implant-ai]] for comparison with Envoy Medical Acclaim and MED-EL TICI

## Strategic Position

### Software-Defined Implants
Cochlear's pivot to upgradeable firmware (Nexa) signals a strategic recognition that future competitive advantage will be defined by deployment pipelines, not hardware lockup. See [[../concepts/software-defined-medical-implants]].

## AI Implications
- 700,000+ devices represent one of the largest in-vivo deployment cohorts for any AI-enabled medical device
- Anonymized field telemetry from this cohort is a uniquely large dataset for sound coding, scene classification, and outcome modeling
- Firmware update infrastructure could enable rapid iteration on ML-driven sound coding strategies
