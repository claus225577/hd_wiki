# Ceretone

OTC/DTC hearing aid brand offering ultra-affordable ready-to-wear hearing aids.

## UK Launch (April 2026)

- **Price:** £129.99 (~$165 USD) — among the cheapest OTC hearing aids in any major market
- **Product type:** Ready-to-wear — preset amplification, no app-based fitting required
- **Market:** United Kingdom
- **Positioning:** Extreme value; marketed as saving consumers thousands vs. traditional hearing aids

## Price Context

| Product | Price | Category |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Ceretone (UK) | £130 / ~$165 | Ultra-affordable OTC |
| Apple AirPods Pro 2 | $249 | Consumer OTC |
| Jabra Enhance | $800-$1,500 | Mid-tier OTC |
| Prescription hearing aid | ~$4,600 | Professional |

At ~$165, Ceretone undercuts even AirPods Pro 2 by ~35%, establishing a new price floor for hearing amplification devices in a major market.

## Strategic Significance

- Demonstrates the OTC price floor is still dropping — from ~$1,600 average to sub-$200
- Raises quality/safety questions: what DNN/DSP capability can exist at this price point? Likely minimal-to-none
- International expansion of affordable OTC beyond the US regulatory framework
- Competitive pressure on NHS hearing aid provision in the UK (NHS provides free hearing aids but with wait times)
- "Ready to wear" (no fitting) is the simplest device category — serves users who cannot or will not use a smartphone app
- Could capture first-time users who would never visit an audiologist or spend $1,000+ on a device

## Open Questions

- What signal processing technology is inside? At £130, likely basic DSP, not DNN
- Does the UK regulatory framework (MHRA) impose the same safety limits as FDA OTC rules?
- What is the return rate and user satisfaction vs. higher-priced OTC devices?
