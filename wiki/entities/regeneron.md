# Regeneron Pharmaceuticals

Regeneron is a biotechnology company that developed Otarmeni (lunsotogene parvec-cwha), the first FDA-approved gene therapy for genetic hearing loss.

## Hearing-Related Products

### Otarmeni (lunsotogene parvec-cwha)
- **Approved:** April 23, 2026 (FDA, via National Priority Voucher — 61-day review)
- **Indication:** Severe-to-profound sensorineural hearing loss caused by biallelic OTOF gene mutations
- **Mechanism:** Dual AAV vector gene therapy injected into inner ear perilymph; delivers functional otoferlin gene
- **Efficacy:** 80% of patients achieved ≤70 dB HL at 24 weeks
- **Pricing:** Free of charge in the United States
- **Significance:** First-ever gene therapy approved for any form of hearing loss

## Relevance to Hearing + AI Ecosystem

Regeneron is not a hearing device company, but Otarmeni's approval represents a new treatment axis for hearing loss that intersects with the hearing technology ecosystem:
- **Potential CI volume impact:** Some pediatric CI candidates with OTOF mutations may receive gene therapy instead
- **Genetic screening pipeline:** AI-driven variant classification will be needed to identify eligible patients
- **Outcome monitoring:** Post-treatment audiometric tracking creates a new data stream for ML analysis
- **Precedent-setting:** Demonstrates pharmaceutical industry entry into hearing loss treatment beyond traditional hearing devices
