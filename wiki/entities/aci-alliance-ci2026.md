# American Cochlear Implant (ACI) Alliance and CI2026 Chicago

The American Cochlear Implant Alliance (ACI Alliance) is a non-profit organization advocating for cochlear implants and improved access. Its biennial scientific meeting — the **CI20XX series** — is the primary US-based scientific gathering on cochlear implantation.

## CI2026 Chicago

- **Dates:** May 7–9, 2026
- **Venue:** Hyatt Regency Chicago, Chicago, IL
- **Audience:** Audiologists, otologic surgeons, speech-language pathologists, psychologists, scientists, engineers, educators, students, advocates
- **Format:** Keynotes, concurrent scientific sessions, panels, posters

### Headline Keynote: Dr. James A. Robinson

The 2024 Nobel Laureate in Economic Sciences (with Daron Acemoglu and Simon Johnson) was announced as a keynote speaker. Robinson's body of work — including "Why Nations Fail" (with Acemoglu) — argues that **institutions** (rules, incentives, distributional politics) determine which populations capture the gains from innovation.

His selection signals that the US CI community is publicly framing access as the field's binding constraint, not technology.

## Why an Institutional-Economics Keynote Matters

CI care has world-class technology and outcomes evidence. The unsolved problem is **utilization**:

- Estimated 5–10% of adult CI candidates in the US receive an implant
- Geographic, financial, reimbursement, and workforce barriers — not device performance — are the binding constraints for most candidates
- International comparisons show 3–5x utilization differences across countries, suggesting institutional rather than biological causes

Robinson's framework is a natural lens for analyzing why some healthcare systems sustainably scale CI access and others don't.

## Implications for Data Science / AI

- **Modeling the system, not just the device:** Outcome data is rich; access pathway data (referral chains, time-to-implant, drop-off points) is much weaker. Significant ML opportunity in mapping where candidates exit the funnel.
- **Health-equity ML:** Predictive identification of underserved CI candidates from existing audiology + EHR data
- **Cross-country comparative ML:** Natural experiments for evidence generation on which institutional changes drive utilization
- **Workforce-system modeling:** Queueing, optimization, and causal inference applied to surgeon/audiologist supply, referral patterns, reimbursement to surface highest-ROI institutional levers
